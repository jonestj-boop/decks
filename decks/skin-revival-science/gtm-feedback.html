Gap 2 · The Missing Persona

Your largest demand pool has no ICP — the mom buying for baby's skin.

The three ICPs cover nursing, pregnancy, and HOCl-curious buyers. But the single biggest search cluster in your keyword universe is baby/infant skin — and no persona owns it. Before this is final, the team needs one decision: is this product for baby's skin, or not?

Proposed ICP 4 · decision needed

"Maya" · The Eczema Mom

Buying for her baby, not herself · ~28–38 · researching at 2am · already failed 2–3 products

Pain points

  • Baby's eczema flares, weeping patches, cradle cap — visible and distressing
  • Steroid-cream hesitancy; wants gentle, safe-near-baby options
  • Pediatrician said "moisturize" — she wants something that actually calms it

Decision factors

Safety on infant skin (non-negotiable) · evidence it soothes · fragrance/steroid-free · pediatric or derm reassurance · reviews from other eczema parents

The catch — and why it's a decision, not a slide. HOCl-for-infant-eczema edges toward a drug claim. If you can't support it, Maya becomes content/SEO authority and top-of-funnel reach — not a product claim. Either way the demand is too big to leave unmapped.

Gap 3 · Competitive Reconciliation

The brands that own the traffic aren't in your square — and your square-mates own no traffic.

The land-grab, proven with data. Frida and Lansinoh own ~405K visits/mo between them but rank for zero HOCl. SkinSmart and Magic Molecule sit in your exact square but draw negligible organic traffic. The HOCl + maternal corner is genuinely unclaimed in search.
Two fixes for the deck. Add Earth Mama and Bodily to the Tier-3 audience map so the competitive set matches who's actually winning the traffic you want — and label SkinSmart/Magic Molecule's organic weakness as the opening it is.
