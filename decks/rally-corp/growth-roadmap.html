Core Web Vitals — What They Mean

LCP — Largest Contentful Paint
Measures the time it takes for the largest above-the-fold element (image, video, or large text block) to render, directly indicating perceived loading speed. A good LCP score is 2.5 seconds or less to ensure a positive user experience and better SEO.
TBT — Total Blocking Time
Measures how long a page is unresponsive to user input (clicks, taps, or key presses) during loading. It quantifies the time between the first content paint and full interactivity when the main thread is "blocked" by long tasks, specifically those lasting longer than 50 milliseconds.
CLS — Cumulative Layout Shift
Measures the visual stability of a webpage by quantifying how much unexpected layout movement occurs during loading. A low CLS score ensures a smooth, stable user experience — elements don't jump around as the page loads.
