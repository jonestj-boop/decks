Go-to-Market Strategy

Three Levers to Drive Growth

State expansion, referral networks, and owned digital channels — layered in sequence for compounding returns.

🗺️

State Expansion

  • Prioritize states with strong HCBS Medicaid programs and high caregiver populations: Pennsylvania, Ohio, Illinois, Florida, Texas
  • Enter one new state per quarter — build the referral network before the content machine
  • Position each state launch with a dedicated landing page + referral outreach sprint
  • Use existing Michigan/Arizona playbooks as repeatable templates
  • Target states where competitor presence is thin — less noise, faster trust
🤝

Referral Partnerships

  • Area Agencies on Aging (600+ across the US) — the single highest-leverage referral source
  • Hospital discharge planners and social workers — catch families at the moment of need
  • Elder law attorneys — serve the same demographic, zero direct overlap
  • Home health agencies — can't themselves be paid through these programs, but refer freely
  • Primary care physicians and geriatricians — trusted authority in the caregiving decision
📱

Digital Owned Channels

  • SEO: rank for high-intent caregiver keywords before paying for clicks
  • Google Ads: capture caregivers actively searching for relief or income
  • Facebook/Meta: reach sandwich-generation adults (35–60) not yet searching but open
  • Email nurture: long consideration cycle — stay present without being pushy
  • Retargeting: site visitors who didn't convert are your warmest audience
Sequencing matters: Launch referral partnerships in a new state first. They deliver free leads while SEO builds. Paid ads bridge the gap during months 2–3 as referral velocity grows.

Paid Ads Strategy

Two Channels. One Goal: Find the Caregiver Before They Give Up.

Google Search captures intent. Meta creates it. Together they cover the full awareness-to-conversion journey.

🔍

Google Search Ads

Targeting Strategy
Campaign 1: Branded Intent
Keywords like "paid family caregiver program," "get paid to care for parent medicaid," "family caregiver stipend near me." Highest intent, best close rate.
Campaign 2: State-Specific Programs
"Michigan Home Help program," "Arizona ALTCS caregiver," "Missouri Medicaid family caregiver." One campaign per active state — tightly geo-targeted.
Campaign 3: Competitor + Alternative
Capture caregivers researching other options: "Medicaid consultant," "how to apply for caregiver pay," "home care Medicaid help." Broad match with negative keywords.
Target CPC: $3–$8 for caregiver keywords. Expected conversion rate: 8–15% from click to eligibility check submission.
📱

Meta (Facebook + Instagram)

Targeting Strategy
Audience 1: Sandwich Generation
Adults 35–60, interests in elder care, caregiving, aging parents. Geo-targeted to active states. Lead-gen ad format with instant eligibility quiz.
Audience 2: Lookalike from Existing Clients
Upload client list to Meta → build 1–2% lookalike audience. Best signal for who actually converts. Start here once you have 100+ approved clients in the list.
Retargeting: Site Visitors + Video Views
Anyone who visited soracare.io without submitting. 30-day window. Ad copy: "Still figuring out if you qualify? Let's check together — takes 3 minutes."
Ad creative: Real caregiver testimonials outperform polished graphics by 3–5x in this category. Start with authentic story-driven video (60–90 sec) shot on phone.

Execution Plan

Your First 90 Days as COO

Foundation before scale. Referral network before ad spend. Organic content before you pay for every click.

Month 1 — Foundation

Know the Numbers & Build the Network

  • Audit current site: technical SEO, page speed, conversion funnel
  • Install GA4 + conversion tracking if not already in place
  • Define ideal client profile — which caregiver converts fastest?
  • Map all 4 state programs: gaps in content, referral coverage
  • Identify 20 target referral partners per state (AAA, social workers, elder law)
  • Begin outreach to top 20 referral partners in highest-volume state
  • Brief a content writer or agency on Pillar 1 awareness content
  • Set up Google Ads account + campaign 1 (branded intent)
Month 2 — Launch

Go Live Across All Channels

  • Publish first 3 Pillar 1 awareness articles (1,500+ words each)
  • Launch state-specific SEO pages for Michigan + Arizona (highest traffic)
  • Go live with Google Search campaigns (campaigns 1 + 2)
  • Launch Meta awareness campaign — sandwich generation targeting
  • Activate retargeting pixel and launch retargeting campaign
  • Host first referral partner webinar or lunch-and-learn (virtual ok)
  • Begin tracking: CPL by channel, lead-to-approval rate, referral volume
Month 3 — Optimize & Expand

Double Down on What Works

  • Review ad performance — kill underperformers, scale winners
  • A/B test ad creative: testimonial video vs. text-based lead ads
  • Publish state pages for Missouri + South Dakota
  • Begin expansion planning: select next state, start referral outreach
  • Launch lookalike audience campaign if client list is large enough
  • Publish 3–5 FAQ articles targeting mid-funnel research queries
  • Build email nurture sequence for leads who don't convert in week 1
  • Establish monthly reporting cadence: leads, CPL, approvals, revenue
The flywheel: Referral partners produce free leads → SEO builds organic volume → Paid ads fill gaps and prove new states → More approved clients → stronger lookalike audiences → lower CPL. Each layer makes the others cheaper.
