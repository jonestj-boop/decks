What we deliberately excluded

Anyone can export a gap report. The value is in what gets cut.

  • Competitor-client university brands. "carlow university" (12,100/mo) and "cmu tepper" (5,400/mo) rank for rival agencies only because those agencies built the schools' sites. Those searchers want a campus, not an agency.
  • Rival agency brand names. Ologie, SimpsonScarborough, RNL. Chasing a competitor's brand term wins a click from someone who already chose them.
  • Macroeconomics definitions. "market economy" pulls 22,200/mo and looks tempting beside an econ-dev pillar. It's students doing homework, not directors hiring an agency.
  • Generic UX education. High volume, wrong buyer.
  • Job-seeker terms. People wanting work at an agency, not people hiring one.
  • Wrong-market geography. NYC and Chicago terms inflate a plan and serve nobody you can serve.

The rest cover Atlanta, your in-state hub, and Seattle, your stated expansion market.
