Why Google flagged this

Velocity signal. Publishing 10+ medication-specific articles in a single week is a textbook scaled AI content pattern. These pages barely ranked before the update wiped them.
YMYL without E-E-A-T. "Best blood pressure medication" and "best antibiotic" are high-stakes medical recommendations. Without author credentials, citations, and reviewer bylines, Google applies maximum skepticism to health-advice content.
Domain trust drag. The March sprint likely pulled down trust signals site-wide — explaining why even older, unrelated pages lost rankings when the update rolled out.
Good news: Pages published mid-2025 are holding significantly better, even in the same topic categories. The foundation is solid; the March sprint is the acute problem.

Recovery Strategy

How We'd Fix This

A three-phase approach: stop the bleeding, rebuild trust, then scale content the right way.

Month 1 — Triage

Stop the Bleeding

  • Full content audit: tag every page as Keep / Fix / Remove
  • Noindex or consolidate March 2026 "best-of" pages that can't be meaningfully improved
  • Add author bylines + credentials to all remaining blog posts
  • Submit recrawl requests via GSC after cleanup
  • Title tag + meta rewrites for top 20 high-impression, low-CTR pages
$3,500 / mo
Month 2 — Rebuild

E-E-A-T Remediation

  • Rewrite the 15 highest-traffic damaged pages with physician review
  • Add clinical citations + "medically reviewed by" schema markup
  • Build an About/Team page establishing medical credentials
  • Differentiate surviving GLP-1 content with original dosing data
  • Internal linking audit — consolidate duplicate topic clusters
$4,500 / mo
Month 3+ — Scale

Sustainable Growth

  • New content brief framework: no publish velocity spikes
  • Prioritize tool/calculator pages (proven to survive updates)
  • Expand Q&A format pages — outperforming blog posts in data
  • Build topical authority in 3–4 core clusters vs. spray-and-pray
  • Monthly GSC monitoring cadence with threshold alerts
$3,000 / mo
Expected outcome: Based on comparable core update recoveries, sites that complete E-E-A-T remediation and content cleanup typically recover 60–80% of lost traffic within 2–3 Google core update cycles (4–6 months).
