02

Two audiences, one domain

The keywords a furniture retailer chases and the keywords a franchise or trade program needs are not the same thing, and treating them as one search strategy is the core issue on adrianahoyos.com today. Consumer-style discovery terms still matter — interior designers behave as prosumers, searching product terms like "sofa bed" or "bedroom ideas" for creative reference long before they ever look for a trade program. Those terms earn a real place in the strategy. But they sit above a second, currently empty layer: people typing "furniture franchise opportunities," "furniture wholesalers," or "interior design trade program" — searches the .com does not appear for at all.

Adriana Hoyos needs both layers working at once: the consumer/prosumer discovery layer it already has some presence in, and the B2B/franchise layer that has to be built from the ground up. Every recommendation in this report is organized around keeping the first and originating the second.

07

How the Website Is Built Is Working Against It

A technical review of adrianahoyos.com identified several structural issues that reduce how well Google can read, understand, and rank the site. Each one matters doubly here — a franchise or trade prospect vetting the brand runs into the same signals Google does.

  • The homepage sends 28 competing signals to Google instead of one

    Every webpage should have one primary headline, called an H1, that tells Google clearly what the page is about. The Adriana Hoyos homepage has 28. When Google sees 28 competing signals, it cannot determine what the page is really about, which weakens the page's ability to rank for anything.

    28 H1 tags found on the homepage, confirmed via JavaScript audit. Best practice is one per page.

  • Google cannot read the showroom locations in a structured way

    There is a standard code format that websites use to tell Google exactly where a physical location is, including its address, phone number, hours, and type of business. Adriana Hoyos does not use this format anywhere on the site. As a result, Google treats the showroom information as regular text rather than business data, which makes it much less likely to appear in local search results and Google Maps.

    No LocalBusiness, Organization, or Product schema markup found on any page reviewed.

  • Dozens of pages compete against each other for the same searches

    When multiple pages on a website all try to rank for the same keyword, they split Google's attention instead of concentrating it. For example, the keyword "adriana hoyos design studio" has more than 20 different pages on the site all competing for it, at positions ranging from 2 to 75. Rather than one strong page ranking well, many weak pages cancel each other out.

    Keyword cannibalization confirmed across multiple terms. "Adriana hoyos dining chairs" competes across 47 separate URLs.

Verified update — Jul 2026

A direct check of Google's index (adrianahoyos.com had been blocking automated fetches, so this required a live-browser crawl) shows the two cannibalization cases have different root causes — and different fixes:

"Dining chairs": the real driver is a sitewide duplicate-content pattern, not a dining-chairs-specific problem. /our-products/dining-room/chairs/ and /ec/our-products/dining-room/chairs/ are near-identical pages competing with each other — and the /ec/ path mirrors the entire product catalog (buffets, tables, chairs, and more), not just this one page. Indexed PDF spec sheets (e.g. RM01-400.pdf, GM02-142.pdf) are also competing in search results alongside the category page. The fix is hreflang tagging (or canonicalization) between the worldwide and /ec/ catalogs sitewide, plus noindexing the spec-sheet PDFs — not a page-by-page redirect exercise limited to dining chairs.

"Design Studio": there is no dedicated Design Studio page on the main domain to consolidate around. The term only appears incidentally — in About Us copy, blog posts, and press releases — not as a real competing landing page. This isn't a "pick a winner and redirect the rest" fix; it's a decision about whether adrianahoyos.com needs a dedicated Design Studio page at all, given that the actual Design Studio content already lives on the separate subdomain being noindexed above.

  • Search result previews are auto-generated, not crafted

    When adrianahoyos.com appears in Google search results, the text that appears below the page title (the description snippet) is pulled automatically by Google because no custom description has been set. This means the brand has no control over its first impression in search results, and the generic text reduces the likelihood that someone will click through.

    Meta descriptions missing on all reviewed pages including the homepage, locations page, and product category pages.

  • No individual pages exist for US showroom locations

    Every location that wants to be found in local search needs its own webpage with its own URL. All US Adriana Hoyos showrooms are currently listed together on a single shared page at adrianahoyos.com/locations/. This page cannot rank separately for each city, cannot be optimized for local keywords, and gets only one visit per month as a result. Competitors like BoConcept have a dedicated page for each of their stores, and those pages rank for local searches individually.

    /locations/ page title reads "Showrooms | Adriana Hoyos" with no city specificity. Zero DC-related keyword rankings.

  • Backlink authority has been declining for 12 months

    The number of external websites linking to adrianahoyos.com dropped from approximately 900 to 657 over the past year, a decline of roughly 27%. Despite prominent press features in publications like Architectural Digest, Elle Decor, and Robb Report, most of those links are marked "nofollow," meaning they pass no ranking benefit to the site. The domain authority score has been flat at 27 out of 100 for the entire year.

    Authority Score 27 (Semrush). Referring domains down from ~900 to 657 as of the original analysis. Most high-authority links are nofollow.

Verified update — Jul 2026

A direct Backlink Analytics pull refines the last finding above: it's two of the three press links that are nofollow, not all three. Architectural Digest links twice (Dec 2025 and Nov 2025 stories), both nofollow. Robb Report links once ("The Table's Edge," Dec 2025), nofollow. Elle Decor's link is already dofollow — a "10 Best Places to Buy Tile Online" piece from Oct 2025 with a Page Authority Score of 34, correctly passing credit today. The ask going forward is narrower than the original finding: request dofollow specifically on future Architectural Digest and Robb Report placements — Elle Decor doesn't need fixing. Referring domains have also kept falling since the original analysis: 578 as of this pull (Domain Overview, Jul 2026), down from 657 in May and roughly 900 a year prior.

Phased recommendations

Sequenced critical-first, structural-second, so SEO and CRO work can run as one coordinated plan.

01Foundation

Fix the architecture that's already broken

Consolidate the homepage to one H1; resolve the sitewide worldwide/.ec duplicate-content pattern and noindex competing PDF spec sheets; decide whether adrianahoyos.com needs its own Design Studio page; noindex the design-services subdomain.

02Build the front door

Stand up the .com B2B hub

Publish a real Trade Program / Become a Franchisee page — staged like Caracole and Bernhardt, with context before the login wall, not a bare gate. Build dedicated individual location pages, starting with the DC and Coral Gables showrooms, and develop trade-program and franchise-opportunity content against the verified keyword clusters.

Implementation checklist

The two phases above, broken into discrete, trackable tasks.

Phase 1 — Foundation

  • Consolidate the homepage's 28 H1 tags to a single canonical H1
  • Implement structured data (schema markup) sitewide — homepage, locations, product pages
  • Write custom meta descriptions for the homepage, /locations/, and product category pages
  • Decide whether adrianahoyos.com needs a dedicated Design Studio page — most "competing" mentions are incidental (About Us, blog, press), not real competitors
  • Implement hreflang (or canonicalization) between the worldwide and /ec/ product catalogs — a sitewide duplicate-content pattern, not limited to dining chairs
  • Noindex PDF spec sheets currently competing with product/category pages in search results
  • Noindex the Design Studio subdomain via robots.txt

Phase 2 — Build the front door

  • Publish a real Trade Program / Become a Franchisee hub on adrianahoyos.com
  • Build dedicated individual location pages, starting with DC and Coral Gables
  • Create trade-program content targeting the verified "interior design trade program" keyword cluster
  • Create franchise-opportunity content targeting "furniture franchise opportunities" and related terms
