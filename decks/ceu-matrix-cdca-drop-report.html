The cause is known and fixable

Root Cause Analysis

What’s Driving the Drop

One primary cause confirmed by both Google Search Console and Google Analytics. Two contributing factors that context the timing.

Primary Cause — Data Confirmed
Root Cause

Duplicate Pages Without a Canonical Tag — Google Cycling Between Them

Your site currently has two live URLs for each CDCA credential — one under /course/ and one under /product/ — with identical content and no signal telling Google which is the authoritative version. Google is forced to choose, and that choice has proven unstable. The data shows this happening simultaneously across both credential pages:

Credential Google’s Current Pick (Apr 22–30) Losing URL Stage
Phase I — Preliminary /product/ — pos 4.7, 61 clicks /course/ — pos 5.5, 24 clicks Both declining
Phase II /course/ — pos 3.4, 4 clicks ↑ switched /product/ — pos 3.8, 7 clicks Preference flipped

The Phase II situation has reversed since the original flag. In March, Google preferred your /product/ page (position 2.9, 56 clicks) over /course/ (position 4.6). As of April 22–30, Google switched — /course/ is now ranking at position 3.4 and /product/ has fallen to 3.8. This cycling is the canonical conflict in action. Neither page wins because the ranking authority that should belong to one URL is permanently divided between two.

The overall position decline Everett flagged in Google Search Console is a direct result of this: both versions are losing ground simultaneously because neither one is accumulating the authority it would hold as the sole canonical page.

Duplicate URL issues that may have been passively tolerated on the old site are now being actively resolved — which is what's producing the cycling behavior visible in the position data.

Action Plan

What EmberTribe Is Doing — and When

Sequenced by priority. Steps 1 and 2 are the critical path. Every day without canonical tags in place allows the drop to continue.

  1. Implement canonical tags on the duplicate CDCA pages Critical — Immediate
    A canonical tag is a single line of code added to the duplicate /course/ pages, telling Google which URL is the definitive version to rank. This stops the cycling and begins consolidating all ranking authority into your /product/ pages.
    /course/ohio-cdca-preliminary-phase-i/ → canonical: /product/ohio-cdca-preliminary-phase-i/
    /course/ohio-cdca-phase-ii/ → canonical: /product/ohio-cdca-phase-ii/
  2. Request re-indexing on all /product/ CDCA pages in Google Search Console Critical — Immediate
    Once canonical tags are live, EmberTribe will request re-crawling of your /product/ CDCA pages via Google Search Console. This accelerates authority consolidation rather than waiting weeks for Google's natural crawl cycle.
  3. Fix the broken link in the Ohio CDCA blog post This Week
    Your top-of-funnel blog post (/cdca-certification-in-ohio-requirements/) contains a broken internal link pointing to a page that no longer exists. This is a missed conversion opportunity — visitors reading the post hit a dead end. EmberTribe will update the link to point to the correct Ohio CDCA product page.
  4. Build the Ohio CDCA credential hub — Month 2 Month 2 — May
    Once canonical tags stabilize positions, the Month 2 content build adds the informational depth these pages need to rank for broader intent queries. Search terms like "cdca requirements ohio" (currently position 8.5, zero clicks) require a dedicated hub page to capture. This is what takes performance from recovery back to March baseline and beyond.
One important note for your development team: Once canonical tags are in place, no new content should be added to the /course/ versions of your CDCA pages. Google will treat them as duplicates going forward, and any content placed there will not be indexed. All content investment should go on the /product/ pages and the new Ohio hub.
