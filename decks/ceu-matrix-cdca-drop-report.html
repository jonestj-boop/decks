Root Cause — Data Confirmed

What's Driving the Drop

One primary cause confirmed by both GSC and GA4. Two secondary factors.

Primary — GSC + GA4 Confirmed
Root Cause

/course/ vs. /product/ Canonical Conflict — Google Switching Preference

Your site has four duplicate URL pairs — one under /course/ and one under /product/ — with identical content and no canonical tag. Google is forced to choose which version to rank, and that choice is unstable. The data shows two different stages of this process happening simultaneously:

Credential Preferred URL (GSC data) Non-Preferred URL Stage
Phase I — Preliminary /product/ — pos 2.9, 65 clicks /course/ — pos 4.6, 16 clicks Mid-transition
Phase II /product/ — pos 2.7, 9 clicks /course/ — pos 4.6, 0 clicks Switch complete

Phase II shows what the endpoint looks like: Google stopped sending traffic to /course/ohio-cdca-phase-ii/ entirely — it has 235 impressions but zero clicks. Meanwhile /product/ohio-cdca-phase-ii/ is holding position but declining. Neither URL is benefiting because the ranking authority that could be consolidated is still split.

The "average position drop" Everett reported in GSC is caused by the /course/ URLs losing position (Phase I: 3.0 → 4.6) while the /product/ URLs aren't fully compensating — resulting in a lower average position across all CDCA queries.

Contributing
Secondary

Post-Migration Crawl Re-evaluation

The new site launched late February 2026. Two months post-migration, Google is still re-assessing the URL architecture. The canonical ambiguity that may have been tolerated on the old site is now being actively resolved — which manifests as the switching behavior we're seeing in the data.

Monitor
Secondary

Google Core Update Window

A confirmed Google update ran in the same timeframe. Core updates typically force Google to act on signals it was previously tolerating — like split ranking authority between two duplicate URLs. The update likely accelerated the canonical switching, not caused a new independent problem. The resolution is identical either way.

Resolution Plan

What We're Doing — and When

Sequenced by priority. Steps 1–2 are in progress this week.

  1. Implement canonical tags on all four /course/ duplicate URLs This Week
    Add <link rel="canonical"> to both /course/ CDCA URLs pointing to their /product/ counterparts. GSC and GA4 both confirm /product/ is Google's preferred URL.
    /course/ohio-cdca-preliminary-phase-i/ → canonical: /product/ohio-cdca-preliminary-phase-i/
    /course/ohio-cdca-phase-ii/ → canonical: /product/ohio-cdca-phase-ii/
  2. Submit all four /product/ CDCA URLs for re-indexing in GSC This Week
    After canonical tags are live, request indexing via GSC URL Inspection on all /product/ CDCA pages. This tells Google to re-crawl immediately and start consolidating authority rather than waiting for the next natural crawl cycle.
  3. Fix the broken internal link on the CDCA blog post This Week
    /cdca-certification-in-ohio-requirements-phase-1-training-and-your-next-steps/ has a broken link to /courses-by-state/ohio (404 — confirmed in site crawl). This post generated 5 organic sessions last week and is a top-of-funnel entry point. The broken link is losing potential conversions. Update the link to point to the correct Ohio product category or the future Ohio hub URL.
  4. Monitor /product/ CDCA page positions weekly Ongoing
    Track /product/ohio-cdca-preliminary-phase-i/ and /product/ohio-cdca-phase-ii/ positions weekly in GSC. After canonical tags are implemented, expect Phase I to hold at position ~2.9. Phase II should recover from the current declining trend as the consolidated signal strengthens. Target: both pages at position 2.5 or better within 4 weeks.
  5. Build the Ohio unified hub — Month 2 cornerstone Month 2
    The /product/ CDCA pages will hold positions once canonicals are fixed, but they're thin on informational content. Queries like "cdca requirements ohio" (pos 7.1 last 7d, not in top 3) require a dedicated credential page. The /ohio-substance-abuse-certification/ hub and /ohio-cdca-certification-requirements/ sub-page are the Month 2 builds that turn this from a recovery into a growth signal.
Do not add content to /course/ CDCA URLs. Once canonical tags point /course/ → /product/, the /course/ URLs will be treated as duplicates and their content will be ignored by Google. All content investment goes on the /product/ pages and the new Ohio hub.
