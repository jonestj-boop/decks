Root Cause — Data Confirmed

What's Driving the Drop

One primary cause confirmed by both GSC and GA4. Two secondary factors.

Primary — GSC + GA4 Confirmed
Root Cause

/course/ vs. /product/ Canonical Conflict — Google Switching Preference

Your site has four duplicate URL pairs — one under /course/ and one under /product/ — with identical content and no canonical tag. Google is forced to choose which version to rank, and that choice is unstable. The data shows two different stages of this process happening simultaneously:

Credential Google’s Current Pick (Apr 22–30) Losing URL Stage
Phase I — Preliminary /product/ — pos 4.7, 61 clicks /course/ — pos 5.5, 24 clicks Both declining
Phase II /course/ — pos 3.4, 4 clicks ↑ switched /product/ — pos 3.8, 7 clicks Preference flipped

The Phase II situation has reversed since the original flag. In March, Google preferred /product/ (pos 2.9) over /course/ (pos 4.6). As of April 22–30, Google has switched — /course/ is now at position 3.4 and /product/ has dropped to 3.8. This is the canonical conflict actively cycling. Neither URL wins because the authority signal is permanently split until canonicals are set.

The continuing decline in average GSC position is caused by both the /course/ and /product/ URLs losing ground simultaneously — no URL is accumulating authority because Google is oscillating between them.

The new site launched late February 2026. Two months post-migration, Google is still re-assessing the URL architecture. The canonical ambiguity that may have been tolerated on the old site is now being actively resolved — which manifests as the switching behavior we're seeing in the data.

The update likely accelerated the canonical switching, not caused a new independent problem. The resolution is identical either way.

Resolution Plan

What We're Doing — and When

Sequenced by priority. Steps 1–2 are the critical path — every additional day without canonical tags allows the drop to deepen.

  1. Implement canonical tags on all four /course/ duplicate URLs Critical — Now
    Add <link rel="canonical"> to both /course/ CDCA URLs pointing to their /product/ counterparts. Note: the Phase II preference has now flipped — /course/ is outranking /product/ as of April 22–30. Canonical tags will consolidate signal into /product/ and stop the oscillation.
    /course/ohio-cdca-preliminary-phase-i/ → canonical: /product/ohio-cdca-preliminary-phase-i/
    /course/ohio-cdca-phase-ii/ → canonical: /product/ohio-cdca-phase-ii/
  2. Submit all four /product/ CDCA URLs for re-indexing in GSC Critical — Now
    After canonical tags are live, request indexing via GSC URL Inspection on all /product/ CDCA pages. This tells Google to re-crawl immediately and start consolidating authority rather than waiting for the next natural crawl cycle.
  3. Fix the broken internal link on the CDCA blog post This Week
    /cdca-certification-in-ohio-requirements-phase-1-training-and-your-next-steps/ has a broken link to /courses-by-state/ohio (404 — confirmed in site crawl). This post generated 2 organic sessions in the April 22–30 window and is a top-of-funnel entry point. Update the link to the correct Ohio product category or the future Ohio hub URL.
  4. Monitor /product/ CDCA page positions weekly Ongoing
    Track /product/ohio-cdca-preliminary-phase-i/ and /product/ohio-cdca-phase-ii/ positions weekly in GSC. After canonical tags are implemented, expect Phase I to begin recovering from position 4.7 back toward 3.3 (March baseline). Phase II should consolidate above position 3.0. Target: both pages at position 2.5 or better within 4 weeks of canonical implementation.
  5. Build the Ohio unified hub — Month 2 cornerstone Month 2 — May
