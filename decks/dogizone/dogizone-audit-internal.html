Technical Problem

Smart Bidding Is Optimizing for the Wrong Things

Five primary conversion action types are active simultaneously. The algorithm treats Get Directions and Store Visits with equal weight to phone call leads, diluting the signal toward low-value actions.

Active Primary Conversion Actions (Feb–May 27)

ActionVolumePriorityShould Be
Local actions - Other engagements 3,011 Primary Remove
Store Visits 1,416 Primary Remove
Calls from Ads 1,102 Primary Primary
Local actions - Directions 955 Primary Remove
Website Calls 496 Primary Secondary*
Reservation Form Completion 424 Primary Primary
Reservation Link Email Submit 272 Primary Secondary
Impact (verified via Google Ads API): Three local/map actions (Other engagements 3,011 + Store visits 1,416 + Directions 955 = 5,382) are all set as primary and outweigh real lead actions (calls + forms = ~2,294) by more than 2 to 1. Smart Bidding is spending most of its optimization effort chasing map interactions, not the calls that create Gingr accounts.

2. Remove Store Visits as account-default goal on lead campaigns
3. Set Book Appointments to secondary on all non-booking campaigns
4. Consolidate phone call primary to a single action (Calls from Ads only until Nimbata live)

Tracking Gap

Call Tracking: The Missing Foundation (Nimbata Selected)

Without call tracking, every campaign optimization is built on incomplete data. The client reviewed the options we shared and chose Nimbata. Option 3 setup was approved May 7. Implementation is the remaining step and is now moving.

What We Can't Measure Until It's Live

No call completion verification. Google counts a call as a conversion when someone taps the phone number. It does not verify the call connected or lasted long enough to qualify as a lead.
No campaign-to-call attribution. Gingr shows 3,236 total inbound calls in March. Google attributed 665. The 2,571 gap cannot be explained without tracking numbers on each campaign.
No call quality data. We cannot assess whether calls from Boarding convert to Gingr at the same rate as calls from Training. Andrew confirmed they do not all convert equally.
No call recording for lead quality. Cannot verify whether calls are from genuine new leads or existing customers calling for service.

Decision: Nimbata — Implementation Plan

Tool options and costs were shared with the client; they selected Nimbata. Next-steps were already sent. Standard implementation sequence:

1. Provision Nimbata account and number pool sized to call volume (~144 hrs/mo)
2. Install dynamic number insertion (DNI) on dogizone.com via GTM; keep the static site number for organic (Option 3)
3. Assign dedicated tracking numbers per ad campaign, all forwarding to the real line
4. Integrate Nimbata with Google Ads + Meta; import qualified calls (60s+) as the primary conversion
5. Validate attribution, then recalibrate Smart Bidding to real call data
Note: Confirm exact Nimbata plan/number-pool cost against the proposal already shared with the client. The 60-second qualifying threshold on Google Ads filters junk calls and keeps tracked minutes down.

Additional Issues

Quick Fixes and Secondary Problems

These items are not the root causes but each degrades performance in a measurable way. Most can be resolved in one work session.

Account-Level Sitelink Contamination

Promotional and service-specific sitelinks set at account level are appearing on unrelated campaigns. Observed in screenshots:

Sitelink Seen OnWrong Campaign
"Sample Saturdays"Boarding search ad
"Group Dog Training Class"Boarding search ad
"Free Training Evaluation"Grooming search ad
"Dog Training Camps"Boarding search ad
"Memorial Day Weekend"Grooming search ad
Fix: Move all promotional and service-specific sitelinks to campaign level. Only universal sitelinks (About Us, Location and Hours) should remain at account level.

Training Search Is One-Legged

All Training Search keyword ad groups are paused. The entire $1,841/month budget runs through a single Dynamic Search Ads (DSA) ad group with uncontrolled query matching. If DSA matching degrades, there is no fallback. Either reactivate 2–3 keyword ad groups or consolidate budget into PMAX Training.

The active Grooming RSA has "Average" ad strength, the lowest of any active search ad. Recommendations: add keywords to headlines and descriptions. Also lost 25.59% impression share to budget in April.

Fix: Refresh Grooming RSA with keyword-rich headlines matching grooming search queries. Target "Good" or "Excellent" rating.

Business Profile Mismatch

Google Business Profile is categorized as "Dog trainer" and this label appears in boarding search ads. A user searching "dog boarding near me" sees "Dog trainer" as the business type, which creates trust friction. Secondary category should be "Pet boarding service."

Fix: Add "Pet boarding service" and "Pet grooming service" as secondary categories in the Google Business Profile. This change propagates to ad extensions automatically.
