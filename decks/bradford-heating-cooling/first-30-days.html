Your First 30 Days

Bradford Heating & Cooling — what happens in Month 1 if you move forward.

The play, in one line

Fix the 400+ pages you've already built so Google finally trusts them, then fill the gaps. You've done the hard part most HVAC companies never do. The problem isn't more content, it's that your pages went up at scale without schema or internal links, and many compete with each other. Month 1 is about making what you have rank, and starting the net-new content that fills your genuine gaps.

Week by week

Week 1 · Fix the foundation

The fastest ranking unlock.

  • Add schema to your pages (right now it's on 0 of 400+) — what earns map-pack placement, rich results, and AI citations.
  • Resolve keyword cannibalization: 64 terms have several of your own good pages chasing them, splitting the relevance. We fix it with internal links and clear page intent, not by deleting pages.
  • Fix the mobile-speed issue (homepage loads ~5.3s on a phone; your customers search on phones in the heat).
  • Clean up the 156 orphaned pages and 17 broken links, and point internal links where they belong.
  • Connect Google Analytics + Search Console so we measure every gain from day one.
Week 2 · Optimize striking-distance pages

The local keywords you already rank #4–20 for (like ac repair surprise at #15, ac repair peoria at #43) get depth, internal links, and schema to push them onto page one. It's the quickest ROI in the whole plan, because the pages already exist and 56 of your local keywords are one push from page one.

Weeks 3–4 · First content batch ships

The first articles go live as drafts for your review, front-loaded on what pays fastest (below).

Where we point the content first

  1. Emergency & same-day. Your biggest winnable gap and your biggest strength: same-day service 7 days a week, and Ken answers the phone. We make you the answer when someone's AC quits at 5 p.m. in July.
  2. Repair vs. replace, and what a new system really costs. Your highest-ticket decisions. We reach homeowners while they're choosing, and position your honest, written-quote approach.
  3. The Sun City & Sun City West homeowner. Two of your cities are among the largest 55+ communities in the country. We speak to them directly: fixed-income repair-or-replace calls, reliability for people home all day in the heat, and the veteran & senior discount you already offer.
  4. Every article links to the service page it supports. The library isn't traffic for its own sake; it's authority funneled straight to the pages that book the job.

About the content plan you're holding

The headlines are working titles — the keyword, angle, and funnel stage for each are the commitment, and your input on the wording is welcome. A few you'll notice (swamp coolers, window/wall units) are there on purpose: they capture searchers and redirect them toward the refrigerated AC and mini-splits you actually install. We deliberately did not rewrite the 24 topics you already have pages for (cost guides, best-brands, heat-pump-vs-AC) — we optimize and link to those instead. As we go, we'll add more retiree-focused topics for Sun City & Sun City West.

How to think about results

Near term
Striking distance + emergency — the fastest wins, because they build on pages that already rank.
Compounding
The net-new gap articles — authority that grows with every Google update, the way our flagship case study went 8,500 → 500,000+ monthly visits with zero algorithm penalties over 4+ years.

Directional, not guaranteed — but this is the order the work pays off in.
