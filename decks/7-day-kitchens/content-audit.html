What's already working — don't touch it

  • Comprehensive structured data — Service, FAQPage, Offer, OfferCatalog, GeoCoordinates, PostalAddress, OpeningHours, and a HomeAndConstructionBusiness (LocalBusiness) profile. Most local remodelers have none of this.
  • Every image has alt text — 0 missing-alt images across all 48 pages. Rare and good for accessibility + image search.
  • Deep money-page content — Kitchen Remodeling (1,928 words), Pricing Guide (3,046), Kitchen Design (1,409). Enough substance to rank.
  • Heavy internal linking — 66–93 internal links per page; your money pages are well-connected (no orphans among the core set).
  • Meta descriptions are well-sized — every core page sits in the 133–168 character sweet spot with a clear local hook.
  • Clear differentiators in the copy — fixed pricing, the 7-day install, and the lifetime warranty are present on the key pages. That's the messaging to lean on in titles too.

Priority fixes

P1 Title tags are too long on almost every core page

10 of your 12 core pages have title tags over 60 characters, so Google truncates them in results — you lose the keyword or the brand at the exact moment a searcher is deciding whether to click. Worst offenders: Kitchen Remodeling (95 chars), Contact (88), Cost Calculator (85), Kitchen Design & Locations (83).
Fix: rewrite each to ≤60 characters, leading with the primary keyword and keeping the brand at the end. Specific rewrites are in the table below. This is the single highest-ROI change — it lifts click-through on pages that already rank.

P1 No Review / AggregateRating schema — you're leaving the stars on the table

You have a Testimonials page and real reviews, but no Review or AggregateRating structured data anywhere on the site. That's why your listings don't show the gold star rating that dramatically lifts click-through for a service business.
Fix: add AggregateRating + Review schema (pulling from Google/Houzz reviews) to the homepage LocalBusiness block and the Testimonials page. This is the one schema type AIOSEO isn't already generating for you.

P1 Homepage has two identical H1s

The homepage outputs "KITCHEN REMODELING IN CHARLOTTE, NC" as an H1 twice. A page should have exactly one H1; duplicates dilute the signal and look like a template bug to crawlers.
Fix: keep one H1, demote the second to an H2 (or style it as a visual heading without the <h1> tag).

P2 The 26 location pages are ~40% templated

Every location page runs ~2,000 words with the same skeleton (Quality Kitchen Remodeling in [City] · Our [City] Services · Why [City] Homeowners Choose Us · Recent Projects · Our History · FAQ). Comparing two cities, ~40% of the sentences are identical with only the city name swapped — e.g. "design-led process with in-house designers, no cookie-cutter packages" appears verbatim on all of them. Google can treat heavily-templated location pages as thin/doorway pages and suppress them.
Fix: raise the genuinely-local share on each. Add real per-city specifics: named neighborhoods, a real local project with photos, local permit/HOA notes, drive-time or "areas we serve within [City]", and a city-specific testimonial. Aim for the boilerplate to be under ~20% of the page.

P2 Kitchen Design page is missing the Service/FAQ schema your other service pages have

Kitchen Remodeling and Kitchen Plus Projects carry Service, Offer, and FAQPage schema. The Kitchen Design page carries none of it — an inconsistency that leaves a core money page without its rich-result eligibility.
Fix: add Service + FAQPage schema to /kitchen-design/ to match the other service pages (and add a short FAQ section if one isn't there).

P3 Thin high-intent pages: Testimonials & Featured Projects hub

Testimonials (306 words) and the Featured Projects hub (375 words) are thin for pages that carry real buying intent and trust signals. The Contact page (332) and Cost Calculator (445) are thin too, but that's acceptable for functional pages.
Fix: expand Testimonials with more full reviews + the AggregateRating schema above; turn the Featured Projects hub into a filterable gallery with an intro paragraph per project type (kitchen, scullery, pantry-laundry) so it targets those searches too.
