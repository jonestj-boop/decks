Your Content Is Working Against You

The content on your blog today works against you, not for you.

You've already published 44 blog posts — proof you'll invest in content. But almost none of it is about fundraising, and to Google that's the difference between building authority and quietly diluting it.

What's on your blog now

Off-target topics. Your articles cover men's health, tech careers, weight loss, unemployment benefits, and recipes — not fundraising, donations, or nonprofits.
AI-generated at scale. No author, no byline, published in bulk on the same dates — the signature of a vendor chasing raw traffic, not your donors and campaign creators.
Monetized for someone else. Several posts end with affiliate links to third-party finance and health products. That traffic earns a commission — not a campaign on your platform.

Why it quietly hurts your rankings

It dilutes your topical authority. Google reads your whole site to answer "what is this site about?" Off-topic signals mean it can't confidently call you a fundraising platform — which is exactly why you sit at position 51-100 for your own category.
It attracts the wrong visitors. The little organic traffic it earns arrives on off-topic searches — health, careers, benefits — people who will never start a fundraiser.
The category leader does the opposite. Donorbox publishes 100% fundraising content; a single guide links to 70+ of its own fundraising articles. Every page reinforces one signal — the compounding authority you're missing.
The fix isn't more content — it's focused content. You've already proven you'll publish. The 120-article plan points that same energy at the searches that bring you donors and campaigns, so every article reinforces one message: you are the fundraising platform.
