01 / Overview

Five phases. One running machine.

Every client moves through the same five phases, regardless of whether they're a podcast, a video series, or both. The phases compress for simpler scopes and stretch for multi-format engines — but nobody skips a phase.

Phase 1

Contract → Kickoff

Contract signed to kickoff call booked. Internal handoff from sales to production.

Phase 2

Discovery & Setup

Brand, voice, tools, and workflow locked. Calendar built.

Phase 3

Foundation Build

Branding, templates, and recording environment go live.

Phase 4

First Cycle

First episode or video produced end-to-end as the proof point.

Phase 5

Cadence & Handoff

Recurring schedule activates. Client moves to steady-state production.

02 / Step by step

From signature to first ship.

Internal handoff & welcome

Sales hands off the signed contract, scope, and any discovery notes to the account lead within 24 hours. Client receives a welcome email confirming next steps and introducing their point of contact.

Owner: Account Lead
  • Create client folder in project management system & shared drive
  • Log scope, cadence, and add-ons from the signed agreement
  • Send welcome email with kickoff scheduling link

Kickoff call

A live call to align on goals, voice, format, and logistics before any production work begins. This sets the creative and operational foundation for everything downstream.

Owner: Account Lead + Producer
  • Confirm format, episode/video length, and segment structure
  • Identify hosts, co-hosts, or talent and their availability
  • Walk through brand assets, tone, and any topics to avoid
  • Set expectations on review windows and approval process

Brand & technical setup

Production builds the infrastructure the client will use for the life of the engagement — recording environment, templates, and naming conventions.

Owner: Producer
  • Set up recording platform (Riverside or equivalent) and test with client
  • Build episode/video templates: intro, lower thirds, outro, thumbnail frame
  • Confirm distribution destinations (YouTube, Spotify, Apple Podcasts, socials)
  • Share client-facing asset request list (logos, photos, music, fonts)

Content calendar & first dates

Build out the production calendar covering the regular cadence and lock the first two recording dates so the team has a concrete target.

Owner: Producer + Account Lead
  • Map recurring cadence (weekly/biweekly/monthly) against the production schedule below
  • Lock first two recording dates and confirm guest/topic pipeline if applicable
  • Share calendar with client for visibility and input

First cycle production

The first episode or video moves through the full pipeline — record, edit, repurpose, review — establishing the rhythm both teams will use going forward.

Owner: Production team
  • Record first session; confirm audio/video quality meets standard
  • Edit full-length asset, then cut short-form clips for distribution
  • Produce show notes, transcript, titles, and thumbnail
  • Route through internal QC before client review

Client review & feedback loop

Client reviews the first deliverable set. This call calibrates taste and pacing so future rounds need less back-and-forth.

Owner: Account Lead
  • Walk client through deliverables and gather feedback in one consolidated round
  • Document specific preferences (pacing, music, caption style, etc.) in the client profile
  • Confirm sign-off and publish/distribution date

Publish & go live

First episode or video ships across every confirmed platform, and the client sees the engine work for the first time.

Owner: Producer
  • Schedule and publish across all confirmed channels
  • Confirm links, metadata, and cross-platform formatting are correct
  • Send client a recap with links and initial performance baseline

Transition to steady-state cadence

Onboarding formally closes. The client moves onto the regular production schedule, with the quick-turnaround track available any time a timely topic calls for it.

Owner: Account Lead + Producer
  • Confirm recurring recording dates for the next 60 days
  • Walk client through how to trigger the quick-turnaround track
  • Schedule first 30-day check-in to review performance and workflow

04 / Production schedule

Two layers, running at once.

Once onboarding closes, every client sits on a layered production schedule: a core cadence that runs on a predictable rhythm, and a quick-turnaround track that activates whenever a time-sensitive topic needs to move fast. They run in parallel without competing for the same production day.

05 / Roles & handoffs

Who owns what, and when it changes hands.

The handoff chain

01
Sales → Account Lead at contract signature. Scope, pricing, and any client-specific commitments transfer in writing.
02
Account Lead → Producer after kickoff. Creative brief, brand assets, and recording logistics transfer into the production calendar.
03
Producer → Editorial/Repurposing after each recording. Raw footage and run-of-show notes move into the edit queue.
04
Editorial → Account Lead for client review. Finished assets route back for sign-off before anything publishes.
05
Account Lead → Client ongoing. Owns the relationship, the 30-day check-ins, and any scope changes for the life of the account.
06 / Success criteria

How we know onboarding worked.

Speed

First deliverable published within 30 days of signature, with no more than one schedule slip.

Clarity

Client can describe their own cadence and how to trigger quick-turnaround without asking.

Calibration

Feedback rounds shrink after cycle one — taste preferences are documented, not re-explained.

Confidence

Client has used or asked about the quick-turnaround track at least once by day 60.
