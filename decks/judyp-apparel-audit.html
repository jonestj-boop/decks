Fix Once, Benefit Forever

One-Time Fixes Do Once

These are structural problems. Fix them once and the improvement compounds indefinitely.

  • 1

    Rewrite title tags + meta descriptions on 6 high-impression pages Critical

    The capsule-wardrobe-basics page has 1.6M impressions at pos 4 with a 0.22% CTR. The title is almost certainly generic. Rewrite to be curiosity-driven and keyword-specific. Same for checklist, hot weather, sustainable clothing, moisture wicking, wrinkle-resistant. This is the single highest-ROI task in this audit.

  • 2

    Push tunic-tops-for-leggings from page 2 to page 1 High

    96,807 impressions at avg position 12.3 — stuck just off page 1. Expand the content (add size guides, styling tips, buyer's guide section), improve the H1/H2 structure, and build 3–5 internal links from the capsule wardrobe and smart casual pages.

  • 3

    Consolidate blog cannibalization between the two blog categories High

    Two categories (judyp-fabrics and my-inspiration) have competing pages: american-made (2+ pages), wrinkle-free (2+ pages), travel clothes (multiple pages). Pick one canonical URL per topic, internally link the others to it, and consider 301 redirects for the weakest duplicate. Google is splitting authority across these.

  • 4

    Build an internal linking hub for the smart casual cluster High

    44 keywords drive 1,115 clicks to the smart casual topic — but they land on multiple URLs with no hub-and-spoke structure. Create a main "smart casual" pillar page and link every related post to it. This consolidates signals and will lift all positions in the cluster.

  • 5

    Add FAQ schema to the top 10 blog posts Medium

    FAQ rich results increase SERP real estate, which boosts CTR without changing ranking position. The tencel, smart casual, and capsule wardrobe posts are natural candidates — they're already answering specific questions.
