Understanding the Gap

Why New Customer Volume Is Below 300

There are three connected reasons. Each is addressable. None of them are about the quality of DogiZone's service or market demand.

📄

Budget Not Fully Spent

  • February through April averaged 80% of the $11,250 monthly budget. That is $2,000+ in unspent ad dollars every month.
  • Brand Search has a $120/day budget set but was spending $7/day because the bidding target was too aggressive.
  • Daycare Search, our most efficient campaign at $17.50 CPL, ran on $50/day when it could absorb $65/day effectively.
  • May is the first month on pace to spend the full budget. We expect this to hold in June.
🔋

Bidding Optimized for Mixed Goals

  • Google's Smart Bidding learns from conversion data. We identified that it was also counting map interactions, directions clicks, and store visits alongside phone calls as equally valuable actions.
  • During the audit period, these local map actions totaled over 5,300 counted conversions, more than double the actual phone calls and form fills. The bidding algorithm treated map taps the same as real leads.
  • When the algorithm optimizes for the wrong mix of actions, it makes budget decisions that do not maximize what Andrew measures: calls that become Gingr profiles.
  • This has been corrected. Phone calls are now the sole primary conversion signal.
📞

No Dedicated Call Tracking

  • There is currently no way to trace a specific call back to the exact campaign or ad that drove it. Google tracks clicks on phone numbers but not whether the call completed or led to a Gingr account.
  • With DogiZone receiving nearly 3,000 inbound calls per month, proper attribution would allow us to redirect budget to the campaigns actually driving the calls that convert.
  • We selected Option 3 (static number on site, dynamic numbers in ads) in May. The tool decision and implementation is the remaining step.
  • This is covered on the next slide.
The upside: All three of these are operational fixes, not strategic overhauls. The market demand is there. The creative and landing pages are strong. The changes in June directly address each of these three gaps.
