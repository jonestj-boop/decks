Understanding the Gap

Why Accounts-Created Volume Is Below 300

There are three connected reasons. Each is addressable. None of them are about the quality of DogiZone's service or market demand.

📄

Budget Not Fully Deployed

  • Through April the account ran under its operating budget most months, leaving planned spend on the table.
  • Several Search and PMAX campaigns had higher daily budgets but were not consuming to the expected amount, so the full budget did not deploy.
  • The Branded campaign's higher budget and aggressive target are intentional (brand protection and efficiency), not a misconfiguration. It spends to available demand.
  • May is the first month at the full operating budget. We expect this to hold.
📡

Tracking & Platform Friction

  • Meta's call conversion event is less reliable than Google's. When call signals are sparse, ad sets fall back into the learning phase, which has forced campaign restarts and slowed Meta's momentum.
  • Dynamic number insertion on the website had display issues, with the wrong phone number showing at times. We are resolving this as part of the Nimbata rollout.
  • Conversion goals were already cleaned up earlier in the engagement. The remaining work is unifying call attribution so the platforms can optimize toward completed calls, the action that creates accounts.
📞

No Unified Call Attribution

  • There is currently no way to trace a specific call back to the exact campaign or ad that drove it. Google tracks clicks on phone numbers but not whether the call completed or led to an account.
  • With DogiZone receiving nearly 3,000 inbound calls per month, proper attribution would let us shift budget to the campaigns actually driving the calls that convert.
  • You selected Option 3 (static number on site, dynamic numbers in ads) and chose Nimbata as the tool. Implementation is the remaining step.
  • This is covered on the next slide.
The upside: All three of these are operational fixes, not strategic overhauls. The market demand is there. The creative and landing pages are strong. The changes in June directly address each of these three gaps.
