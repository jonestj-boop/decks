Strategy · Site Architecture

Locations Pages as the Hub of Residential SEO

The residential strategy is built on a hub-and-spoke architecture. Each market gets a location hub, each hub anchors service-area pages beneath it, and every blog post links up into the right hub. This is how a flooring site ranks locally and builds topical authority at the same time.

📍 Tier 1 — Location Hubs

  • /madison/residential-flooring — the Madison conversion page
  • /milwaukee/residential-flooring — the Milwaukee conversion page
  • Each hub: showroom info, services, product range, reviews, book-a-visit CTA
  • These are the pages we want ranking for "flooring [city]" searches

🏘️ Tier 2 — Service-Area Pages

  • Madison: Middleton, Fitchburg, Sun Prairie, Verona, Waunakee
  • Milwaukee: Brookfield, Wauwatosa, Waukesha, Mequon
  • Each links up to its metro hub and out to relevant guides
  • Captures suburb-level "near me" intent the big boxes ignore
Why hubs win: Location pages convert (they have showroom info + CTAs), but they're thin on content signals alone. Blog posts are content-rich but don't convert. Internally linking the two gives Google both relevance signals at once — and gives the buyer a clean path from "researching" to "booked." The next slide shows exactly how the links flow.
Strategy · Internal Linking

How Location Pages & Blog Posts Link Together

Every ClusterMagic article links up to the relevant location hub; every location hub links down to its supporting articles. This two-way flow passes ranking authority and routes ready-to-buy traffic into the pages that convert.

The rule we follow: No orphan blog posts. Every article published is mapped to a parent location hub before it goes live, with at least one contextual link up to that hub and one to a relevant product/booking page. That's what turns content volume into local rankings.
