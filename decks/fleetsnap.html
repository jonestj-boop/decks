Appendix · Technical SEO

Strong foundation, a few real gaps

✅ Already done well

  • solidDynamic rendering serves crawlers full HTML: unique titles, meta descriptions, and canonicals per page.
  • solidOne clean H1 per page, plus rich JSON-LD (WebApplication, FAQ, HowTo, Breadcrumb) and per-page OG images.
  • solidCoverage reaches Google, Bing, GPTBot, ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude and the social scrapers. Sitemap is current.

🔧 The remaining gaps

  • P1Homepage isn't prerendered to anyone. Your #1 brand page (28% CTR) still serves the generic shell: no unique title, canonical, H1, or schema.
  • P2Crawler access is gated by user-agent. robots.txt itself is permissive and correctly allows GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, Bing and Google — but full HTML is served only to recognized crawler UAs, so anything unrecognized (and the homepage, for everyone) still falls back to the empty shell.
  • P2Move from UA-gated dynamic rendering to true SSR / static prerender so every bot and user gets the HTML.
  • P2Validate the AggregateRating schema (damage-claims, concierge). Star ratings must be real and on-page or risk a manual action.
The takeaway for Ari: the on-page and rendering work you shipped in June is genuinely strong, so this isn't a rebuild. It's closing the homepage and AI-search-bot gaps, then hardening the rendering approach. Quick, high-leverage fixes on a good base.

Guides Review · the verdict

The /guides are built for operators — the search bet just isn't proven yet

✅ What's right

  • intentEvery guide is framed for scaling hosts: "fleet," "managing more than a couple cars," "scaling past 10 vehicles." This is the opposite of the calculator crowd.
  • topic fitCo-hosting, partner statements, VAs, AI automation, and damage claims map directly to the $800–$2K concierge offer.
  • proven"p&l statement for turo host" already ranks position 1.9 — on just 15 impressions, but it's exactly the operator language the guides use.

⚠️ What to watch

  • no dataThe guides have 0 impressions and 0 clicks in 90 days — they're new and not yet indexed at volume. The intent read is structural, not yet confirmed in GSC.
  • mismatchTwo titles risk pulling the wrong audience: "turo monthly rentals" reads as a renter query, and the guest-messaging study sits near guest-side "message a host" searches (which GSC confirms are renters).
  • leakThe pages that do get traffic (calculators) don't yet funnel into these guides.

Bottom line: the content is pointed at the right buyer. The work is getting it indexed, linked, and pointed away from two ambiguous terms — then proving the mix shift in GSC over the next quarter.
