Paid Media Strategy

Three Channels. One Funnel.

$9,000/mo in ad spend across Meta, Google, and LinkedIn — each channel plays a specific role.

G

Google Ads

$3,000/mo • Bottom of Funnel

  • Capture high-intent searches: "CDCA certification online," "addiction counselor CEU courses"
  • Branded defense — prevent competitor conquesting
  • State-specific campaigns (Ohio first, then expand)
  • Shopping/product-style ads for course packages
Why Google first: People already searching for certifications have the highest purchase intent. We meet them at the moment of decision.
in

LinkedIn Ads

$3,000/mo • Mid-Funnel

  • Target by credential acronyms (CDCA, LCDC, CADC, etc. in job titles)
  • Target social workers, counselors, substance abuse professionals
  • Lead magnet: free CE guide or renewal checklist for email capture
  • Retarget site visitors with renewal reminders
Why LinkedIn: Professionals literally put their certification acronyms in their titles. Unmatched targeting precision for this niche.
f

Meta Ads

$3,000/mo • Top of Funnel

  • Awareness campaigns: career guides, certification pathways
  • Lookalike audiences built from customer email list (5-10K emails)
  • Video + static creative testing (short-form education content)
  • Retargeting: site visitors, video viewers, email engagers
Why Meta: Widest reach at lowest CPM. We cast the awareness net, capture emails, and feed those audiences back to Google and LinkedIn.
Low competition confirmed. We audited the Facebook Ad Library for all major competitors. Only CE4Less (~65 ads) and NetCE (~24 ads, nursing-focused) are active on Meta. Zero competitors run LinkedIn ads. In the addiction counseling CE niche specifically, paid social is nearly uncontested — your $9K/mo will go further than in a saturated market.
No ad history = learning phase. Expect 2-4 weeks before algorithms optimize. We'll test creative formats (static, video, carousel), copy lengths, and audience segments. Month 1 is data collection. Month 2 is optimization. Month 3 is scaling winners.
