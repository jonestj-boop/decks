Executive Summary

What We Found

OtterSpace has a genuinely strong product with excellent social proof - 5-star ratings across 150-200+ reviews, a patented mechanism, OEKO-TEX certification, and three distinct customer segments actively searching for this solution: Parents trying every sleep hack for their children, Sleep Optimizers who treat sleep as a performance lever (Whoop, Huberman Lab listeners), and Shift Workers who need blackout during daylight hours. The CRO issues are not about the product but about how the site communicates trust, reduces friction, and guides shoppers toward checkout. The primary funnel has several significant gaps that are actively costing conversions: stale preorder dates that destroy credibility, trust information (returns, guarantees, shipping timelines) that exists but isn't surfaced on product pages, and a homepage that under-delivers on the brand's value proposition for all three segments. The good news: most of these are quick Shopify fixes, copy additions, or page-linking changes - not full redesigns.

Critical Issues: Fix Immediately

1
Preorder Products Showing a December 2024 Ship Date Critical Outer Curtain PDPs Collection Page
The Outer Curtain products (Bloom, Midnight, Space Otters, Dinos, Cloud White) are listed as "EXCLUSIVE PREORDER - LIMITED QUANTITIES AVAILABLE" with the message "Pre-orders expected to ship in early December 2024." It is currently April 2026 - 16+ months after that deadline. Any shopper who reads this will immediately question whether the company is legitimate, whether orders will be fulfilled, or whether the site is abandoned. This is the single highest-priority issue on the entire site.
How to Fix
  • If the products are now in stock and shipping: remove all preorder messaging immediately. Update product descriptions, variant labels, and any references to December 2024. Change the "EXCLUSIVE PREORDER" badge to something like "In Stock" or remove it entirely.
  • If still on preorder: update the ship date to a realistic current estimate and add a transparency note explaining the delay (e.g., "We're working through our waitlist - thank you for your patience. Estimated ship: [Month Year]").
  • If discontinued: remove the products or mark clearly as unavailable with a redirect or "notify me" option.
  • Audit all product pages for similar stale date references before publishing any ad traffic.
2
Negative Inventory on Live Products (-32 Units Showing) Critical Outer Curtain 84"
The Outer Curtain Bloom 84" variant shows -32 inventory in the product data while still being listed as available for purchase. This means 32 orders may have been taken for stock that doesn't exist. Customers who ordered could receive delayed or unfulfilled shipments, leading to chargebacks, negative reviews, and damage to the brand.
How to Fix
  • Audit inventory counts in Shopify admin for all outer curtain variants immediately.
  • Cross-reference actual warehouse stock against Shopify inventory records.
  • If oversold: proactively email affected customers with a timeline and offer a discount or alternative.
  • Enable Shopify's "Continue selling when out of stock" toggle only intentionally and with accurate lead times displayed on the PDP.
  • Set up a low-stock alert threshold in Shopify (or a third-party tool) to prevent this happening again.
3
Out-of-Stock Variants Shown With No Redirect or Alternative Critical Multiple PDPs Collection Page
Multiple variants are out of stock across the catalog (91" Blackout Kit, 63" and 95" Outer Curtain Bloom, most Outer Curtain sizes). These appear greyed out but with no "notify me when back in stock," no messaging about expected restock, and no redirect to an alternative size or product. A shopper who lands on a product needing a specific size and finds it unavailable has no path forward - they leave.
How to Fix
  • In Shopify admin, go to Products > [product] > Variants and confirm each OOS variant has "Continue selling when out of stock" turned OFF. Then install a back-in-stock app - Swym Back in Stock or Klaviyo (if already installed) both integrate natively with Shopify and add a "Notify Me" button automatically when a variant is unavailable.
  • Add a short copy line next to greyed variants: e.g., "Restocking soon - join the waitlist."
  • On the collection page, consider hiding variants that are entirely out of stock from the default view, or at minimum label them clearly so they don't kill browsing momentum.
5
FAQ Page Exists But Is Not Linked From Product Pages Critical All Product Pages Nav
There is a full FAQ page at inotterspace.com/pages/faqs but it is not linked from the main navigation or from any product page. GA4 confirms this page converts at 5.45% - more than twice the 2.21% site average. It is the highest-converting page on the entire site. Customers who find it buy at 2.5x the rate of average visitors, yet it receives only 110 sessions out of 19,128 because nothing links to it. This is the fastest ROI fix available.
How to Fix
  • In Shopify admin: Online Store > Navigation > Main menu > Add menu item. Set label to "FAQ" and link to /pages/faqs.
  • On product pages: Themes > Customize > Product page > Add block > Collapsible row. Add 4-6 FAQ rows per product covering installation, sizing, light blocking %, travel use, and washing. This is a native Shopify feature - no app needed.
  • At minimum, add a text link in the product description: "Have questions? See our full FAQ >"

High Priority: Fix Before Next Paid Campaign

4
No Return Policy or Guarantee Visible on Product Pages High All Product Pages
Blackout curtains are a considered purchase - customers want to know they can return the product if it doesn't work for their windows. There is no mention of a return window, refund policy, or satisfaction guarantee anywhere visible on product pages. The most common question before any first purchase is "what if it doesn't work?" This is unanswered.
How to Fix
  • In Shopify admin, go to Online Store > Themes > Customize, open a product page, and look for a "Custom HTML" or "Text" block below the Add to Cart button. Add a trust badge row there: Free Returns · 30-Day Guarantee · Free Shipping · OEKO-TEX Certified. If your Aurora theme supports metafields, you can also use a product-level metafield block.
  • Add a collapsible accordion block in the product description using Shopify's Themes > Customize > Product page > Add block > Collapsible row. Label it "Shipping & Returns" and link to your Shopify policy pages under Settings > Policies.
  • If you offer a satisfaction guarantee, lead with it: "Try it risk-free for 30 days." This single line can meaningfully lift conversion on a premium-priced item.
6

There is a size guide at inotterspace.com/pages/size-guide but it is not linked from the variant selector on any product page. The most common reason a shopper hesitates on a curtain purchase is uncertainty about sizing. If they pick the wrong size, they return it. If they're unsure which size, they abandon.   • If the size guide page has good visuals (measuring diagrams, window type examples), link to it from the announcement bar or homepage too.

How to Fix
  • In Themes > Customize > Product page, add a Text or Custom HTML block below the Add to Cart button with the message: "Pay with your HSA or FSA card at checkout - pre-tax savings apply." Link to /pages/hsa-fsa-eligibility. Include it in the trust badge row alongside Free Returns and OEKO-TEX.
  • Add "HSA/FSA Eligible" as one of the trust badge row items below the Add to Cart button.
  • Consider a dedicated callout section on the homepage targeting the sleep-health audience: parents, shift workers, travelers - the same audiences your blog targets.
  • Add FAQ to the hsa-fsa page: "What counts as a qualifying expense? How do I submit for reimbursement?"

Homepage Lacks a Clear Above-Fold Value Proposition High Homepage
The homepage converts at 1.17% CVR despite receiving 52% of all site traffic. The product page it should be sending people to converts at 2.94% - 2.5x higher. GA4 shows 1,040 people left the homepage without viewing a single product. The page has no strong H1, no subheadline, and no primary CTA - it is doing navigation-level work when it should be doing sales-level work. OtterSpace has three distinct customer segments (Parents, Sleep Optimizers/Health Enthusiasts, and Shift Workers) - the current homepage doesn't speak to any of them.
How to Fix
  • Lead with an outcome-first H1 that spans all three segments: "The Last Blackout Curtain You'll Ever Need" or "Total Darkness. On Every Window." Avoid parent-specific language in the hero - that's one segment, not the whole market. Reference designs: eightsleep.com (outcome-led, science-forward, dark and sleek) and trygraymatter.com (premium, clean, health-performance feel) are the right tone to aim for.
  • Add a subhead explaining the mechanism and the unique edge: "The only kit that seals light from the sides, top, and middle - not just the fabric." This directly addresses the #1 failure point of competitors like Sleepout (see Issue #23).
  • Add a prominent CTA button ("Shop Blackout Kits") and a hero image showing the product installed in a real bedroom.
  • Below the hero: a 3-icon trust row (✓ 100% Blackout Guarantee · ✓ Tool-Free Install · ✓ OEKO-TEX Certified) followed by segment-specific proof - one quote from a parent, one from a shift worker, one from a sleep-focused customer - to signal "this product is for you" to all three audiences without spelling it out explicitly.

The vast majority of visitors (typically 95-98%) don't purchase on the first visit. There is no email popup, exit-intent offer, footer newsletter signup, or lead magnet anywhere on the site. This means every non-purchasing visitor is lost permanently with no way to follow up.

  • For the back-in-stock out-of-stock visitors: capture emails via "Notify Me" (see Issue #3) - these are high-intent leads.
  • The HSA/FSA page is also a natural lead capture opportunity: "Get a reminder before your FSA deadline" opt-in.

26
GA4 Tracking Bug: Variant Revenue Not Attributed Correctly High GA4 / Shopify Integration
The view_item and add_to_cart events fire with the full variant name (e.g., "Total Blackout Curtain Kit - 57" / One Panel") but the purchase event fires with just the base product name ("Total Blackout Curtain Kit"). As a result, every variant shows $0 revenue and 0 purchases in GA4. The 57" kit has 6,290 views and shows $0 - while the base product name records all 357 purchases and $64,677. You cannot currently tell which sizes are selling, which are dead weight, or where to focus inventory. Fix this before making any variant-level decisions.
How to Fix
  • In your Shopify GA4 integration (or Google Tag Manager), locate the purchase event tag and confirm the item_name parameter matches the format used in view_item and add_to_cart.
  • The fix is typically setting item_name to include the variant title consistently across all three events - either always include the variant suffix, or always use just the base product name. Pick one and apply it to all events.
  • After the fix, verify in GA4 > E-commerce Purchases that variant-named items now show purchase data.
  • Once fixed, re-run the E-commerce Purchases report over 30+ days to get clean variant-level revenue data to inform inventory and merchandising decisions.

Product pages appear to have primarily one hero product image, with limited lifestyle or room-context shots. Before/after comparisons (light leaking vs. sealed) are especially compelling for this product category.
How to Fix
  • For each product: add a minimum of 5-8 images including: (1) product on white background, (2) installed in a real room, (3) close-up of the magnetic/edge sealing mechanism, (4) the blackout effect in a dark room, (5) travel/portability use case.
  • Source customer photos from your review platforms (Loox especially) - you likely already have great UGC that can be added to the gallery.
  • A before/after GIF or short video clip of the light-blocking effect would outperform any static image.
18
Kit vs. Bundle Differentiation Is Unclear Medium Homepage Collection Page
There is a "Total Blackout Curtain Kit" ($129-149) and separate "Blackout Curtain Bundles" ($151-354). First-time visitors will not immediately understand the difference between these two offerings, which can lead to confusion, wrong product selection, and abandonment. The bundle includes an outer decorative curtain - but this isn't telegraphed clearly on the collection page.
How to Fix
  • Add a short comparison section on the homepage or collection page: "What's the difference? Kit = blackout layer only. Bundle = blackout layer + decorative outer curtain."
  • On the collection page, add a subtitle or secondary text under each product name clarifying what's included.
  • Consider adding a "Which is right for me?" quiz or decision-helper - even a 2-option selector ("I just need blackout" vs. "I want style + blackout") that routes to the correct product.
19
Dr. Melissa Smith (PhD Founder) Is an Underused Trust Signal Medium About Page Homepage PDPs
The about page title references "Melissa Smith, Ph.D. | Founder of OtterSpace Sleep Solutions." A PhD founder in a sleep-science context is a significant credibility differentiator - especially for HSA/FSA-eligible products positioned as health tools. This information appears to be on a page that most shoppers never visit and is not surfaced on product pages or the homepage.

  • Reference her credentials in the PDP copy: "Developed by Dr. Melissa Smith, Ph.D., to solve the exact problem she faced as a new parent."

20
Three Review Platforms Running Simultaneously Medium Site-Wide
Loox, Judge.me, and Yotpo are all installed and loading on the site. Each adds JavaScript payload and potential widget conflicts. Reviews are fragmented across three platforms - a product showing 203 reviews on Judge.me may show 78 on Yotpo, undermining perceived social proof. Also, three paid review apps is an unnecessary monthly cost.
How to Fix
  • In Shopify admin: Apps > [review app to remove] > Delete app. Before deleting, export your reviews: in Judge.me go to Settings > Import/Export > Export. In Loox go to Settings > Export reviews. Import into your chosen platform first, then delete the others.
  • After removing unused apps, go to Themes > Edit code and check for any leftover widget snippets (look for Loox or Yotpo liquid tags in product templates) and remove them to clean up load time.
21
Page Speed Risk From Heavy Third-Party Script Load Medium Site-Wide
The site loads a significant number of third-party scripts: Hotjar, TikTok Pixel, Facebook Pixel, Google Tag Manager, TriplePixel, three review platforms, Kaching Bundles, Gorgias chat, Klaviyo, and more. Each adds weight. Estimated LCP (Largest Contentful Paint) is likely in the 2.5-3.5s range. Every 1-second delay in page load correlates with a 7% drop in conversions (industry benchmark). On mobile this is amplified.
How to Fix
  • Run the site through Google PageSpeed Insights and prioritize any LCP or CLS warnings.
  • Remove the two unused review apps (see Issue #20) - this is the easiest quick win.
  • Use Google Tag Manager to load non-critical pixels (TikTok, TriplePixel) after the page is interactive, not on initial load.
  • Ensure product images are served in WebP format and properly sized for the container - oversized images are a common Shopify slowdown.
27
Award Placement: "Parents Best for Baby 2026" Needs Careful Positioning Medium Homepage PDPs
OtterSpace won Parents Best for Baby 2026: Best Blackout Curtains - a legitimate third-party credibility signal. The risk: prominently displaying a "Best for Baby" award in the hero or above-fold sends a category signal to non-parent shoppers (shift workers, sleep optimizers) that this is a children's product. That's one of three customer segments, not the whole market. The award's value as social proof must be captured without letting it define the brand's positioning for everyone who isn't a parent.
How to Fix
  • Do not place it in the hero or immediately below the product title. The first impression on a 32-year-old shift worker or Huberman Lab listener should not be a baby-specific award badge.
  • Place it in a "Press & Awards" or "As Seen In" trust bar - ideally alongside other credentials (OEKO-TEX, HSA/FSA eligible, any press mentions). In a row of 4-5 trust signals, it reads as external validation rather than a product category statement. The shopper registers "this brand is credible" rather than "this is a baby product."
  • Use it prominently on nursery-specific pages and blog posts (e.g., the nursery blog post, any baby/nursery collection if one exists). Full badge, full context - it's perfectly on-brand there.
  • In Klaviyo, use it in email flows targeted at the parent segment (customers who mention babies or nurseries in their order notes or who came from nursery blog posts). It's a powerful close for that audience.
  • Framing if used in the main trust bar: consider displaying just the award logo/badge image (without the "Best for Baby" text) if the image format allows, or using small supporting copy: "Award-winning sleep solution - Parents Magazine 2026." This gives credit without the category narrowing.

No Competitive Differentiation on Site - Shoppers Are Comparing Elsewhere Low Homepage / PDP
OtterSpace competes in a well-defined category with three main direct competitors: Sleepout (suction cup portable blackout curtains - but suction cups fall, and the product blocks fabric light only, not edge gaps, leaving customers disappointed); UBlockout (premium aluminum-framed custom shades, genuinely 100% blackout, but $500-600 per window - roughly 5x OtterSpace's price); and GuardBlinds (similar concept to UBlockout, lower price, but questionable quality and overseas manufacturing). None of these objections or differentiators are addressed anywhere on inotterspace.com. Shoppers who arrive mid-funnel after comparing Sleepout will have specific doubts ("does it actually seal the edges?") that go unanswered.
How to Fix
  • Add a comparison table on the PDP or homepage: OtterSpace vs. Sleepout vs. UBlockout. Key axes: actual light seal (edges + top + middle), price, installation method (no-drill vs. screws), return policy, and portability. OtterSpace wins on all four for the renter/traveler audience.
  • Address the "does it actually block edge gaps?" doubt directly in the product copy - this is the exact failure point Sleepout customers complain about. A before/after image or short video of a window with no edge leak is the most persuasive asset possible.
  • Greenguard certification is coming - once live, this directly counters Sleepout's primary "safest for kids" angle. Plan to update the PDP and homepage trust row the day it's certified.
  • UBlockout's $500+ price point is a gift - a subtle "starting at $X vs. $500+ for custom shades" callout positions OtterSpace as the smart, renter-friendly choice without sounding defensive.

How to Fix

25
Announcement Bar Is Doing Too Much at Once Low Site-Wide
The announcement bar rotates through three messages: HSA/FSA eligibility, "Up to 20% OFF," and "Free Shipping." Rotating bars are often ignored (banner blindness) and each message competes with the others. "Up to 20% OFF" without context can feel vague and salesy.
How to Fix
Pick one primary message per campaign/season and make it specific. "Free shipping on all US orders" is more credible than "Up to 20% OFF." Or lead with urgency when running a real promo: "Sale ends Sunday - 15% off all kits." Test single-message vs. rotating in GA4.
