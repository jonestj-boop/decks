02 -Executive Summary

Party Kids America has a functional e-commerce rental site with real inventory, fair pricing, and a legitimate service area -but the site is leaving a significant amount of revenue on the table through several structural conversion blockers.

The single most damaging issue is the payment model: the site accepts only cash or PayPal upon delivery, with no online credit card checkout. For a product that requires advance booking and a $50 deposit, this creates immediate hesitation. Most customers in 2026 expect to pay by card at the time of booking to confirm their reservation -the current model shifts all the commitment onto the customer with no reciprocal confirmation from the business.

Beyond payments, the site struggles with zero visible social proof, a hero section that conveys no value proposition, and critical purchase information buried in an FAQ page instead of surfaced at the point of decision. There are also structural UX gaps -the availability calendar requires too many clicks to reach, delivery costs are hidden until late in the funnel, and product pages lack the details needed to justify the spend.

The good news: most of these fixes are execution-level changes, not rebuilds. The product catalog is solid, pricing is competitive, and the service differentiation (Texas-inspected, no hidden fees, free delivery zones) is genuinely strong -it just isn't showing up where it needs to.

04 -Issues by Priority
Critical — Fix these first. Each one is actively blocking or destroying purchase intent.
C-01
No online credit card payment -cash or PayPal upon delivery only
Checkout High Effort
The Problem
The FAQ explicitly states: "We accept Cash or PayPal upon delivery." There is no option to pay by credit or debit card at time of booking. For a rental service that requires customers to plan days or weeks ahead, this is a fundamental trust gap. A customer who can't pay by card has no confirmed booking -just a verbal promise. Most modern customers in this category expect to pay and confirm online, the same way they'd book Airbnb, a bounce house on a competitor site, or any other service requiring advance scheduling.
The Fix
Integrate Stripe (or Square) for card payment at checkout. Collect the $50 deposit online at time of booking. Cash and PayPal can still be offered for the remaining balance on delivery. If a full payment processor integration isn't feasible immediately, even a "secure deposit by card" option through Stripe Checkout or a hosted payment link would be a meaningful improvement. This is the single highest-ROI change on this list.
How to fix in GoHighLevel
  1. Go to Settings > Payments > Integrations and connect your Stripe account. GHL has native Stripe support with no third-party plugin needed.
  2. In Payments > Products, enable "Collect payment at checkout" for each rental. Set the $50 deposit as a required payment at time of order.
  3. In the Website Builder, edit the checkout page and add a GHL Order Form element. Under payment settings, set it to "partial payment" mode so customers pay the deposit now and the balance on delivery.
  4. Enable Apple Pay and Google Pay under Settings > Payments > Payment Methods - critical given 56% of your traffic is iOS.
  5. Test the full checkout flow on an iPhone in Safari before going live.
C-02
Zero customer reviews or ratings visible anywhere on the site
Sitewide Low Effort
The Problem
Neither the homepage, category pages, product pages, nor checkout display any customer reviews, testimonials, star ratings, or Google review counts. For a service business where strangers are inviting equipment into their home or event, social proof is a primary trust mechanism. A parent booking a bounce house for their kid's birthday party wants validation that other parents have done this safely and had a good experience. The absence of any visible reviews creates doubt -not neutrality.
The Fix
1. Add a Google Reviews widget or manually embed 5-8 best reviews (with star rating, name, date) directly on the homepage and each product page. 2. Display a total review count and average star rating in the header or above the fold ("4.8 - 200+ Google Reviews"). 3. Add a testimonials section on the homepage above the fold, ideally with a photo of the actual event setup. Even a static section with 3 real quotes is a significant conversion lift over nothing.
How to fix in GoHighLevel
  1. In the Website Builder, add a new section to your homepage. Use the "HTML/Custom Code" element to embed a Google Reviews widget (Elfsight or EmbedSocial both work with GHL). Paste the embed code directly into the element.
  2. For a no-code option, go to Reputation > Reviews in GHL - copy your best 5-8 reviews and manually build a testimonial section using GHL's card or column elements.
  3. On each product page in the Website Builder, scroll to below the "Book Now" button and add a 3-column row with one testimonial quote per column.
  4. In the Website Builder > Header, add a small trust line with your Google star rating and review count. Style it as plain text - e.g. "4.8 stars - 200+ Google Reviews."
C-03
Hero section has no value proposition -rotating carousel with no headline
Homepage Med Effort
The Problem
The homepage hero is a rotating image carousel with no persistent headline, no stated value proposition, and no qualifying statement about who the service is for. Visitors arriving from Google, social, or referrals have to infer what the business does from photos alone. Carousels have decades of documented evidence of poor conversion performance -users don't watch them, banner blindness kicks in, and they hide content from everyone except those who happen to see the first slide.
The Fix
Replace the carousel with a single static hero image (your best setup photo) overlaid with a clear headline. Suggested format:
// Hero headline structure H1: "Party Rentals in Pearland -Delivered, Set Up & Cleaned Up" Subhead: "Bounce houses, water slides & concessions for birthdays, schools & corporate events. Serving SE Houston." CTA: "Browse Rentals" | "Check Availability"
This immediately tells who you are, where you serve, what you offer, and what to do next in under 3 seconds.
How to fix in GoHighLevel
  1. In the Website Builder, open your homepage and delete the existing slider/carousel element.
  2. Add a new Section at the top with a full-width background image (upload your best event photo via Media Library). Set a dark overlay at 40% opacity so text is readable.
  3. Inside the section, add a Heading element for your H1, a Paragraph for the subheadline, and a Button Row with two buttons: "Browse Rentals" linking to /category/ and "Check Availability" linking to /order-by-date/.
  4. In the section settings, set min-height to 520px and center-align content vertically. Save and preview on mobile before publishing.
C-04
Delivery fee is hidden -not shown until late in the checkout funnel
Cart / Checkout Low Effort
The Problem
The FAQ states delivery fees are charged separately based on distance. But the site claims "NO hidden fees" prominently in the About page. The disconnect between that promise and discovering a delivery fee during checkout is a classic cart abandonment trigger. Surprise costs at checkout are the #1 reason customers abandon e-commerce carts, and this applies equally to rental bookings.
The Fix
Add a delivery fee calculator or lookup widget on every product page so customers can enter their zip code and see the delivery fee instantly before adding to cart. Alternatively, display a static delivery fee table on product pages ("Free delivery to Pearland, Manvel, Rosharon. $XX to Sugar Land, Missouri City, etc."). Also update the "no hidden fees" claim to say "transparent pricing - see delivery fee before you book" so it's accurate and still reassuring.
How to fix in GoHighLevel
  1. In the Website Builder, open each product page and add a new row below the product title/price area.
  2. Add a Text/HTML element containing a simple delivery fee table. Format it as a two-column list: Zone / Fee. Example: "Pearland, Manvel, Rosharon - Free | Sugar Land, Missouri City - $XX | Houston (beyond 20mi) - $XX."
  3. If you want a zip code calculator, use GHL's Form element with a single zip code field that submits to a custom workflow. The workflow looks up the zip and sends an SMS/email with the exact fee. This is a medium-effort build inside Automation > Workflows.
  4. Update the "no hidden fees" text on the About page in the Website Builder to say "Transparent pricing - delivery fee shown on every product page before you book."

H-01
Rental duration (6 hours) and key booking terms buried in FAQ
Product Pages Low Effort
The Problem
The FAQ reveals that all rates are for a 6-hour rental. This is important context a customer needs before deciding whether a $425 or $699 item is worth it -but it's only visible if they navigate to the FAQ page. Similarly, the $50 nonrefundable deposit requirement for orders over $300 is a purchase condition that customers should see before adding to cart, not after. Hiding these details creates surprise and distrust at critical decision moments.
The Fix
Add a persistent "Rental includes 6-hour window - $50 deposit required for orders over $300 - Cancel for weather with full credit" callout on every product page, ideally directly below the price. Also add an inline FAQ accordion on product pages with the 4-5 most common questions (duration, delivery, cancellation, setup requirements) so customers never have to leave the page to resolve objections.
How to fix in GoHighLevel
  1. In the Website Builder, open a product page. Just below the price element, add a Text element with a light-gray background box containing: "6-hour rental window - $50 deposit for orders over $300 - Free rescheduling for weather." Style it at 13px, muted color.
  2. To add an FAQ accordion, use GHL's built-in Accordion element (found in the element panel under "Content"). Add it below the Book Now button. Pre-populate with: How long is my rental? / Is delivery included? / What's the cancellation policy? / What surfaces do you set up on? / What power do I need?
  3. Repeat for every product page, or build one product page template in the Website Builder and duplicate it so the callout and FAQ carry forward automatically.
H-02
No filtering or sorting on category pages -customers can't narrow by need
Category Pages Med Effort
The Problem
The Bounce Houses page has 13 products ranging from $200 to $425. The Water Slides page has 22 products from $290 to $699. Customers shopping for a specific age group, theme (princess, dinosaur, superhero), or budget have to scroll every product manually. Parents planning a kid's birthday party are time-constrained and theme-specific -if they can't quickly surface the Paw Patrol or princess bounce house, they'll go to a competitor who makes it easy.
The Fix
Add filter/sort controls to category pages: Sort by (Price: Low to High, Price: High to Low, Popular), Filter by Theme (Princess, Dinosaur, Sports, Neutral), Filter by Type (Dry/Wet), and Filter by Price Range. Even basic theme tags displayed on product cards (colored badge: "Princess," "Dinosaur," "Sports") help customers self-qualify quickly without building a full filter system.
How to fix in GoHighLevel
  1. GHL's native product catalog does not support front-end filtering. The quickest workaround is to create separate category sub-pages by theme: /category/princess-bounce-houses/, /category/dinosaur-bounce-houses/, etc. Link to these from the main category page with visual tile buttons.
  2. In each product listing in Payments > Products, add a custom field for "Theme" (Princess, Dinosaur, Sports, Neutral, Water). This won't power a filter but organizes your catalog for when you build sub-pages.
  3. For a sort-by-price solution, duplicate the category page twice - one sorted low-to-high and one high-to-low - and add a visible "Sort by Price" toggle at the top that links between the two pages.
  4. Add theme badge labels to product card images directly in the Website Builder using a small colored tag overlay on each product thumbnail.
H-03
"Order By Date" availability check requires too many clicks and is buried
Homepage / Nav Med Effort
The Problem
Checking availability is one of the first things a motivated customer wants to do -"Is my date even open?" -yet the "Order By Date" flow requires navigating to a separate page, clicking a button to open a calendar modal, waiting for AJAX to load, and is blocked entirely if cookies are disabled. This is a high-intent action being gated behind unnecessary friction. The date check should be the first thing a visitor can do, not the fourth.
The Fix
Add an inline date picker directly in the hero section of the homepage: "What's your event date?" - calendar input - "See Available Rentals" button. This surfaces availability checking at the moment of highest intent, before they've even browsed products. Also consider adding a persistent date selector to the top of each category page so availability filtering is always one step away.
How to fix in GoHighLevel
  1. In the Website Builder, open the homepage hero section. Add a Form element with a single Date field labeled "What's your event date?" and a submit button labeled "See Available Rentals."
  2. Set the form's redirect URL to your /order-by-date/ page (or /category/) with the selected date passed as a URL parameter if your booking system supports it.
  3. Under Automation > Workflows, create a simple workflow triggered by the form submission that stores the date as a contact field - useful for follow-up if they don't complete a booking.
  4. For category pages, add the same date form element at the top of each category page template in the Website Builder.
H-04
No urgency or scarcity signals -customers have no reason to book now
Product Pages / Category Low Effort
The Problem
Party rentals are inherently time-constrained -popular summer weekends and holiday dates book out weeks in advance. The site makes no mention of this at the product or category level. Customers browsing in April for a May party feel no urgency to book today, even though the best equipment genuinely may not be available if they wait. Missing urgency signals is a particularly costly gap for a seasonal, inventory-constrained service.
The Fix
Add date-aware urgency copy to product pages once inventory data is available (e.g., "Only 2 summer Saturdays left in June"). In the near term, add seasonal urgency banners: "Summer weekends book fast - secure your date early." On product pages, add copy like "This unit books 2-3 weeks out for weekend events." Also consider a "Popular" or "Hot This Season" badge on bestselling items for social proof-driven urgency that doesn't require live inventory data.
How to fix in GoHighLevel
  1. In the Website Builder, add a thin announcement bar at the top of your site (above the header) with seasonal copy: "Summer weekends are filling up - book your date now." Update this manually at the start of each season.
  2. On each product page, add a small Text element directly below the price with copy like: "Books 2-3 weeks out for weekend events - secure your date early."
  3. For "Popular" badges, open each top-selling product page in the Website Builder and add a colored label element overlaid on the product image. Use a bright color (red or orange) with the text "Popular" or "Books Fast."
  4. Add the same badge to product cards on category pages by editing the product thumbnail image directly in Media Library - overlay the badge in a photo editor before uploading.
H-05
Trust signals exist on the About page but are invisible at point of purchase
Product Pages / Checkout Low Effort
The Problem
The About page contains strong trust content: Texas Department of Insurance inspection, sanitized before/after every use, no hidden fees, family-owned local business, commitment to weekends and holidays. None of this is visible on product pages or in the checkout flow -where customers are actually deciding whether to complete a purchase. Trust signals do zero work sitting on a page most visitors never see.
The Fix
Add a 4-icon trust bar below the "Book Now" button on every product page and in the checkout sidebar:
✓ Texas Dept. of Insurance Inspected ✓ Sanitized Before & After Every Use ✓ Free Setup & Breakdown Included ✓ Family-Owned, Locally Operated
These are genuine differentiators that reduce risk perception right at the decision point.
How to fix in GoHighLevel
  1. In the Website Builder, open a product page and add a new 4-column row directly below the "Book Now" button.
  2. In each column, add an Icon + Text element: checkmark icon with one trust line each (Texas DOI Inspected / Sanitized Before & After / Free Setup & Breakdown / Family-Owned).
  3. Style the row with a light gray background, small font (13px), and centered alignment. Save as a Section Template in GHL so you can drop it onto every product page without rebuilding.
  4. For the checkout page, add the same trust row in the right sidebar column of the order form layout.
H-06
Inconsistent CTA language -"Book Now" and "Reserve Now" used interchangeably
Sitewide Low Effort
The Problem
Product listing cards use "Book Now" while the navigation header uses "Reserve Now." These lead to different destinations (product page vs. category page), but the inconsistency creates subtle confusion -customers may wonder if clicking a different button takes them somewhere different or initiates a different kind of commitment. Inconsistent CTAs signal a lack of intentionality that subtly erodes trust.
The Fix
Standardize on one CTA verb sitewide. "Book Now" is the stronger choice - it's more action-oriented and implies commitment (which is what you want). Use "Book Now" on all product cards, product pages, and the primary header CTA. Reserve "Check Availability" as the secondary CTA for date-first browsing flows. Every instance of "Reserve Now" should become "Book Now."
How to fix in GoHighLevel
  1. In the Website Builder, open the site header. Find the primary nav button labeled "Reserve Now" and change the text to "Book Now." Save and publish.
  2. Go to each category and product page in the Website Builder and update any button elements that say "Reserve Now" to "Book Now."
  3. If the "Reserve Now" label is coming from a global navigation element, update it once in the Global Sections or header template and it will apply sitewide automatically.
H-07
"Clear Order" button in cart is too prominent -high accidental click risk
Cart Low Effort
The Problem
The cart page displays a "Clear Order" button that resets the entire cart. This is placed near the checkout CTAs with no confirmation dialog. A customer who accidentally clicks this loses everything they've selected -and on mobile, touch targets are close together. A single accidental tap that empties the cart is a guaranteed lost conversion, especially if the customer is in a hurry or on a small screen.
The Fix
Two changes: 1) Add a confirmation modal before clearing ("Are you sure you want to remove all items from your cart? This can't be undone.") 2) Move or visually demote the "Clear Order" button - make it small, gray, and text-only, far from the "Checkout" CTA. Destructive actions should never compete visually with primary conversion actions.
How to fix in GoHighLevel
  1. The cart page in GHL is partially controlled by the platform's native checkout system. Open the cart page in the Website Builder and locate the "Clear Order" button element.
  2. If the button is a custom GHL element, add an onclick confirmation via custom JavaScript in the page's Custom Code section (Settings > Custom Code): document.querySelector('.clear-order-btn').addEventListener('click', function(e) { if (!confirm('Remove all items from your cart?')) e.preventDefault(); });
  3. To visually demote the button, edit its style in the Website Builder: set the background to transparent, text color to light gray (#999), font size to 12px, and add significant margin to separate it from the Checkout button.
H-08
No lifestyle photos on product pages -customers can't visualize the rental at their event
Product Pages Med Effort
The Problem
Product pages currently show catalog-style images of equipment on its own. Parents booking a bounce house for a kid's birthday party make emotionally-driven decisions — they want to picture it at their backyard party, see kids having fun, and feel confident it will look great. Stock-style product shots don't trigger that emotional response. Competitors who show real event photos create significantly stronger purchase intent.
The Fix
Add 2-3 real event photos to every product page — images of the item set up at an actual party, ideally with kids using it. These don't need to be professional; authentic iPhone shots from real deliveries perform well. Build a secondary image gallery below or alongside the primary product image. Prioritize high-traffic product pages first (water slides, bounce house combos, mechanical bull).
How to fix in GoHighLevel
  1. Upload event photos to your Media Library in GHL. Name them descriptively (e.g., "water-slide-backyard-party-pearland.jpg") for SEO benefit.
  2. In the Website Builder, open each product page and add an Image Gallery element below the primary product image. Set it to display 3-4 images in a horizontal scroll or lightbox format.
  3. For the primary product image slot, consider replacing the catalog shot with your best real-event photo of that item — it will immediately feel more authentic and trustworthy.
  4. Caption each event photo with a brief line: "Setup in Pearland, TX — 8-year-old birthday party." This builds local trust and specificity.
H-09
No product videos -highest-converting media type is completely absent
Product Pages Med Effort
The Problem
Video is the single highest-converting media format in e-commerce — customers who watch a product video are significantly more likely to purchase. For party rentals, a static image of an inflatable can't convey how large it actually is, how fast it inflates, how excited kids get using it, or how it looks fully set up in a real backyard. These are the exact objections a parent needs resolved before booking a $400+ item for their child's birthday.
The Fix
Start with 3-5 short videos (15-30 seconds each) for the highest-traffic products: top water slides, most popular bounce houses, and the mechanical bull. Shoot on an iPhone in landscape mode at real deliveries. Show: (1) setup process timelapse, (2) kids actually using it, (3) close-up of size/scale with an adult nearby for reference. Upload to YouTube or embed directly. A single authentic clip per product will outperform any written description.
How to fix in GoHighLevel
  1. Upload videos to YouTube (unlisted is fine) or host on Vimeo. Do not host large video files directly in GHL's Media Library — it's not optimized for video delivery.
  2. In the Website Builder, open a product page and add a Video element below the product image. Paste the YouTube or Vimeo URL. GHL renders it as an embedded player automatically.
  3. Set the video thumbnail to a compelling still frame (kid laughing on the slide, not a black screen). In YouTube, set a custom thumbnail before embedding.
  4. For product pages where you don't yet have a video, add a placeholder section with text: "Watch it in action — video coming soon" to signal that content is forthcoming and set expectations.

M-01
No upsell or bundle strategy on product pages -only suggested items in cart
Product Pages Med Effort
The Problem
