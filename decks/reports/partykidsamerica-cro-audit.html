02 -Executive Summary

Party Kids America has a functional e-commerce rental site with real inventory, fair pricing, and a legitimate service area -but the site is leaving a significant amount of revenue on the table through several structural conversion blockers.

The single most damaging issue is the payment model: the site accepts only cash or PayPal upon delivery, with no online credit card checkout. For a product that requires advance booking and a $50 deposit, this creates immediate hesitation. Most customers in 2026 expect to pay by card at the time of booking to confirm their reservation -the current model shifts all the commitment onto the customer with no reciprocal confirmation from the business.

Beyond payments, the site struggles with social proof that exists on the homepage but disappears on product pages and checkout, a hero section that conveys no value proposition, and critical purchase information buried in an FAQ page instead of surfaced at the point of decision. There are also structural UX gaps -the availability calendar requires too many clicks to reach, delivery costs are hidden until late in the funnel, and product pages lack the details needed to justify the spend.

The good news: most of these fixes are execution-level changes, not rebuilds. The product catalog is solid, pricing is competitive, and the service differentiation (Texas-inspected, no hidden fees, free delivery zones) is genuinely strong -it just isn't showing up where it needs to.

C-01
No online credit card payment -cash or PayPal upon delivery only
Checkout High Effort
The Problem
The FAQ explicitly states: "We accept Cash or PayPal upon delivery." There is no option to pay by credit or debit card at time of booking. For a rental service that requires customers to plan days or weeks ahead, this is a fundamental trust gap. A customer who can't pay by card has no confirmed booking -just a verbal promise. Most modern customers in this category expect to pay and confirm online, the same way they'd book Airbnb, a bounce house on a competitor site, or any other service requiring advance scheduling.
The Fix
Integrate Stripe (or Square) for card payment at checkout. Collect the $50 deposit online at time of booking. Cash and PayPal can still be offered for the remaining balance on delivery. If a full payment processor integration isn't feasible immediately, even a "secure deposit by card" option through Stripe Checkout or a hosted payment link would be a meaningful improvement. This is the single highest-ROI change on this list.
How to implement in ERS
  1. ERS uses its native payment processor ERSPay for card transactions. Contact ourers.com support to activate ERSPay on your account — this enables online credit and debit card collection at checkout without any third-party integration.
  2. Once ERSPay is active, go to Admin > Deposit Rules (/cp/deposit_rules/) and set a deposit of either a fixed dollar amount or percentage of the order total. This deposit will be collected at the time of booking.
  3. In Admin > Misc Order Settings (/cp/misc_order_settings/), configure the balance collection method — customers can pay the remaining balance on delivery while the deposit is captured online at booking.
  4. Apple Pay and Google Pay availability depends on ERSPay's supported payment methods — confirm with ourers.com support whether wallet payments are enabled for your ERSPay account.
  5. Test the full checkout flow on an iPhone in Safari before going live to confirm the deposit collection and confirmation email trigger correctly.
C-02
Reviews exist on the homepage but are missing from product pages and checkout -where decisions happen
Product Pages / Checkout Low Effort
The Problem
The homepage has a "What Customers Are Saying" section with a Google Reviews carousel — that's a good foundation. But reviews are completely absent from category pages, product pages, and checkout. That's the problem: social proof is showing up on the discovery page but disappearing exactly when a customer is evaluating a specific $400 rental and deciding whether to book. A parent reading a water slide product page has no reviews in front of them at the moment they need reassurance most. Trust signals that aren't at the point of purchase aren't doing their job. Additionally, there's no aggregate rating (e.g. "4.8 ★ — 200+ reviews") in the site header to set confidence from the first impression.
The Fix
1. Pull 2-3 of the strongest reviews from the homepage carousel and embed them directly on each product page below the price/CTA area. These don't need to be product-specific — general 5-star experiences build trust for any booking decision. 2. Add an aggregate star rating + review count to the site header or directly below the hero ("4.8 ★ · 200+ Google Reviews") so it's visible on every page from the first second. 3. Consider converting the homepage carousel to a static 3-card grid — carousels hide content from users who don't interact with them, and the reviews are valuable enough to show all at once.
How to implement in ERS
  1. Go to Products > Items (/cp/items/), open each item, and add 2-3 review quotes directly in the item description field using simple HTML: reviewer name, star rating (★★★★★), and review text. ERS item descriptions render HTML, so this will display cleanly on every product page without a third-party widget.
  2. To add the aggregate star rating + review count to the header, go to Website > Responsive Editor (/cp/responsive_editor/) and use the Custom Code section to inject a trust line into the header: "4.8 ★ · 200+ Google Reviews" styled in a small, high-contrast font. This appears sitewide on every page load.
  3. To convert the homepage reviews carousel to a static 3-card grid, go to Website > Website Pages (/cp/website_pages/), open the homepage, and replace the carousel block with a 3-column HTML grid of hardcoded review cards — name, stars, date, and quote text. Faster to load, impossible to miss.
  4. If you want the full Google Reviews feed on product pages (auto-updating), use Elfsight or EmbedSocial to generate an embed script scoped to show your top 3 reviews. Paste the script in the item description HTML for your highest-traffic products first (water slides, bounce house combos, mechanical bull).
C-03
Hero section has no value proposition -rotating carousel with no headline
Homepage Med Effort
The Problem
The homepage hero is a rotating image carousel with no persistent headline, no stated value proposition, and no qualifying statement about who the service is for. Visitors arriving from Google, social, or referrals have to infer what the business does from photos alone. Carousels have decades of documented evidence of poor conversion performance -users don't watch them, banner blindness kicks in, and they hide content from everyone except those who happen to see the first slide.
The Fix
Replace the carousel with a single static hero image (your best setup photo) overlaid with a clear headline. Suggested format:
// Hero headline structure H1: "Party Rentals in Pearland -Delivered, Set Up & Cleaned Up" Subhead: "Bounce houses, water slides & concessions for birthdays, schools & corporate events. Serving SE Houston." CTA: "Browse Rentals" | "Check Availability"
This immediately tells who you are, where you serve, what you offer, and what to do next in under 3 seconds.
How to implement in ERS
  1. Go to Website > Responsive Editor (/cp/responsive_editor/). ERS uses pre-built header templates — select one of the "Cover Background" header options (e.g. option 5, 8, or 9) which support a full-width hero image with text overlay. This replaces the carousel layout.
  2. Upload your best event photo to Website > Gallery (/cp/gallery/) and set it as the header background image in the Responsive Editor header settings.
  3. In the Responsive Editor's Custom Code section, add your H1 headline and subhead as HTML overlaid on the hero. Alternatively use the ERS header text fields if the chosen template supports editable headline copy.
  4. Add two CTA buttons in the hero via the Custom Code section linking to your category page and the availability/order-by-date page. Preview on mobile before saving.
  5. ERS also has a Conversion Booster (/cp/conversion_booster/) with split testing — use it to A/B test the new hero against the current carousel before fully committing.
C-04
Delivery fee is hidden -not shown until late in the checkout funnel
Cart / Checkout Low Effort
The Problem
The FAQ states delivery fees are charged separately based on distance. But the site claims "NO hidden fees" prominently in the About page. The disconnect between that promise and discovering a delivery fee during checkout is a classic cart abandonment trigger. Surprise costs at checkout are the #1 reason customers abandon e-commerce carts, and this applies equally to rental bookings.
The Fix
Add a delivery fee calculator or lookup widget on every product page so customers can enter their zip code and see the delivery fee instantly before adding to cart. Alternatively, display a static delivery fee table on product pages ("Free delivery to Pearland, Manvel, Rosharon. $XX to Sugar Land, Missouri City, etc."). Also update the "no hidden fees" claim to say "transparent pricing - see delivery fee before you book" so it's accurate and still reassuring.
How to implement in ERS
  1. The good news: ERS already has your delivery fees configured. Go to Admin > Service Areas (/cp/service_areas/) — 111+ cities are listed with travel fees and minimum order amounts. The data exists, it just isn't surfaced on product pages.
  2. To display fees on product pages, go to Products > Items (/cp/items/), open each item, and add a brief delivery fee summary in the item description field: "Delivery: Free to Pearland, Manvel, Rosharon · $XX to Sugar Land, Missouri City · contact us for areas beyond 20mi."
  3. For a sitewide delivery fee reference page, go to Website > Website Pages (/cp/website_pages/), create or edit a "Delivery Areas" page, and list zones and fees pulled from your Service Areas data. Link to this page from every product page description.
  4. Update the "no hidden fees" text on the About Us page via Website > Website Pages to say "Transparent pricing — delivery fee shown before you book."

H-01
Rental duration (6 hours) and key booking terms buried in FAQ
Product Pages Low Effort
The Problem
The FAQ reveals that all rates are for a 6-hour rental. This is important context a customer needs before deciding whether a $425 or $699 item is worth it -but it's only visible if they navigate to the FAQ page. Similarly, the $50 nonrefundable deposit requirement for orders over $300 is a purchase condition that customers should see before adding to cart, not after. Hiding these details creates surprise and distrust at critical decision moments.
The Fix
Add a persistent "Rental includes 6-hour window - $50 deposit required for orders over $300 - Cancel for weather with full credit" callout on every product page, ideally directly below the price. Also add an inline FAQ accordion on product pages with the 4-5 most common questions (duration, delivery, cancellation, setup requirements) so customers never have to leave the page to resolve objections.
How to implement in ERS
  1. Go to Products > Items (/cp/items/), open each rental item, and add a short callout at the top of the item description: "6-hour rental window · $50 deposit for orders over $300 · Free rescheduling for weather." This appears on the product page for every visitor.
  2. For an inline FAQ on product pages, add the 4-5 most common questions and answers directly in the item description field using simple HTML formatting (bold question, plain text answer). ERS item descriptions support HTML.
  3. To apply consistently across all items, update one item first, then copy the callout and FAQ HTML block into each remaining item description in Products > Items.
  4. Alternatively, use Website > Source Code (/cp/source_code/) to inject a global FAQ block that appears on all product pages via a CSS/JS selector targeting the product page template.
H-02
No filtering or sorting on category pages -customers can't narrow by need
Category Pages Med Effort
The Problem
The Bounce Houses page has 13 products ranging from $200 to $425. The Water Slides page has 22 products from $290 to $699. Customers shopping for a specific age group, theme (princess, dinosaur, superhero), or budget have to scroll every product manually. Parents planning a kid's birthday party are time-constrained and theme-specific -if they can't quickly surface the Paw Patrol or princess bounce house, they'll go to a competitor who makes it easy.
The Fix
Add filter/sort controls to category pages: Sort by (Price: Low to High, Price: High to Low, Popular), Filter by Theme (Princess, Dinosaur, Sports, Neutral), Filter by Type (Dry/Wet), and Filter by Price Range. Even basic theme tags displayed on product cards (colored badge: "Princess," "Dinosaur," "Sports") help customers self-qualify quickly without building a full filter system.
How to implement in ERS
  1. Go to Products > Categories (/cp/categories/) and create sub-categories by theme: Princess Bounce Houses, Dinosaur Bounce Houses, Sports, Water, etc. ERS supports nested category structures that map to separate browsable pages on the site.
  2. In Products > Items (/cp/items/), assign each item to its appropriate theme sub-category using the Secondary Category field (visible in the items list). Items can belong to multiple categories in ERS.
  3. Use Products > Sorting (/cp/sorting/) to control the display order of items within each category — put bestsellers and most popular items first.
  4. After creating sub-categories, update the navigation via Website > Navigation Editor (/cp/navigation_editor/) to link to each theme sub-category from the main category page or nav dropdown.
H-03
"Order By Date" availability check requires too many clicks and is buried
Homepage / Nav Med Effort
The Problem
Checking availability is one of the first things a motivated customer wants to do -"Is my date even open?" -yet the "Order By Date" flow requires navigating to a separate page, clicking a button to open a calendar modal, waiting for AJAX to load, and is blocked entirely if cookies are disabled. This is a high-intent action being gated behind unnecessary friction. The date check should be the first thing a visitor can do, not the fourth.
The Fix
Add an inline date picker directly in the hero section of the homepage: "What's your event date?" - calendar input - "See Available Rentals" button. This surfaces availability checking at the moment of highest intent, before they've even browsed products. Also consider adding a persistent date selector to the top of each category page so availability filtering is always one step away.
How to implement in ERS
  1. ERS's "Order By Date" availability picker is a native feature of the platform. The date selector widget can be embedded on any page using ERS's built-in embed shortcode or widget tag — contact ourers.com support for the specific embed code to place the availability picker in the homepage hero.
  2. Go to Website > Responsive Editor (/cp/responsive_editor/) and use the Custom Code section to inject the availability date widget into the homepage hero area, positioned below the headline and above the product grid.
  3. For category pages, go to Website > Website Pages (/cp/website_pages/), open each category page, and add the availability embed at the top of the page content so customers can filter by date without leaving the category view.
  4. Use Website > Conversion Booster (/cp/conversion_booster/) to run a split test comparing the current "Order By Date" link in nav vs. the inline date picker in the hero — let data confirm the lift before rolling out sitewide.
H-04
No urgency or scarcity signals -customers have no reason to book now
Product Pages / Category Low Effort
The Problem
Party rentals are inherently time-constrained -popular summer weekends and holiday dates book out weeks in advance. The site makes no mention of this at the product or category level. Customers browsing in April for a May party feel no urgency to book today, even though the best equipment genuinely may not be available if they wait. Missing urgency signals is a particularly costly gap for a seasonal, inventory-constrained service.
The Fix
Add date-aware urgency copy to product pages once inventory data is available (e.g., "Only 2 summer Saturdays left in June"). In the near term, add seasonal urgency banners: "Summer weekends book fast - secure your date early." On product pages, add copy like "This unit books 2-3 weeks out for weekend events." Also consider a "Popular" or "Hot This Season" badge on bestselling items for social proof-driven urgency that doesn't require live inventory data.
How to implement in ERS
  1. Go to Website > Responsive Editor (/cp/responsive_editor/) and use the Custom Code section to inject a thin announcement bar above the header with seasonal copy: "Summer weekends are filling up — book your date now." Update each season via the same Custom Code field.
  2. Go to Products > Items (/cp/items/) and add urgency copy to each item description directly below the price area: "Books 2-3 weeks out for summer weekends — secure your date early."
  3. For "Popular" or "Books Fast" badges, upload product thumbnail images with a badge overlay pre-applied (add in any image editor) to Website > Gallery (/cp/gallery/), then update the item image in Products > Items. ERS displays item images on category listing pages automatically.
  4. Check Website > Conversion Booster (/cp/conversion_booster/) heatmap data to confirm which product cards get the most attention on category pages — prioritize those items for "Popular" badge treatment first.
H-05
Trust signals exist on the About page but are invisible at point of purchase
Product Pages / Checkout Low Effort

The About page contains strong trust content: Texas Department of Insurance inspection, sanitized before/after every use, no hidden fees, family-owned local business, commitment to weekends and holidays. None of this is visible on product pages or in the checkout flow -where customers are actually deciding whether to complete a purchase. Trust signals do zero work sitting on a page most visitors never see.

✓ Texas Dept. of Insurance Inspected ✓ Sanitized Before & After Every Use ✓ Free Setup & Breakdown Included ✓ Family-Owned, Locally Operated
These are genuine differentiators that reduce risk perception right at the decision point.
How to implement in ERS

  2. For a sitewide approach, go to Website > Source Code (/cp/source_code/) and inject a CSS-styled trust bar that targets the product page template and appears automatically on every item page without editing each item individually.
  3. For the checkout page specifically, use Website > Website Pages (/cp/website_pages/) to find the checkout/order page and add the trust bar HTML in the page content area.
H-06
Inconsistent CTA language -"Book Now" and "Reserve Now" used interchangeably
Sitewide Low Effort
The Problem
Product listing cards use "Book Now" while the navigation header uses "Reserve Now." These lead to different destinations (product page vs. category page), but the inconsistency creates subtle confusion -customers may wonder if clicking a different button takes them somewhere different or initiates a different kind of commitment. Inconsistent CTAs signal a lack of intentionality that subtly erodes trust.
The Fix
Standardize on one CTA verb sitewide. "Book Now" is the stronger choice - it's more action-oriented and implies commitment (which is what you want). Use "Book Now" on all product cards, product pages, and the primary header CTA. Reserve "Check Availability" as the secondary CTA for date-first browsing flows. Every instance of "Reserve Now" should become "Book Now."
How to implement in ERS
  1. Go to Website > Navigation Editor (/cp/navigation_editor/) and update any nav button labeled "Reserve Now" to "Book Now." Changes in the Navigation Editor apply sitewide automatically.
  2. Go to Products > Items (/cp/items/) and check the CTA button label in each item's settings — update any instance of "Reserve Now" to "Book Now."
  3. Go to Website > Website Pages (/cp/website_pages/) and search each page's content for "Reserve Now" — update to "Book Now" wherever found, particularly on the homepage and category pages.
  4. Use Website > Source Code (/cp/source_code/) to do a find-and-replace across any global template sections if "Reserve Now" is hardcoded into the site theme.
H-07
"Clear Order" button in cart is visually too prominent -sits too close to the Checkout CTA
Cart Low Effort
The Problem
The cart page has a "Clear Order" button that resets the entire cart. A confirmation dialog does exist ("Are You Sure You Want To Reset Your Entire Order?") — so the worst-case accidental wipe is already guarded against. The remaining issue is visual hierarchy: the button carries too much visual weight given how destructive it is, and it sits too close to the "Checkout" CTA. On mobile, where touch targets are close together, it still creates unnecessary friction and anxiety near the final conversion action.
The Fix
Visually demote the "Clear Order" button — make it small, gray, and text-only with no background or border, positioned well away from the "Checkout" CTA. Destructive actions should look minor compared to the primary conversion action. The confirmation dialog can stay as-is.
How to implement in ERS
  1. To visually demote the button, add a CSS rule in Website > Source Code (/cp/source_code/) targeting the Clear Order button: set font-size: 12px; color: #999; background: transparent; border: none; and add margin to push it well away from the Checkout CTA. Inspect the cart page HTML to confirm the exact button class or ID selector first.
  2. If ERS has a native setting for cart page layout, check Admin > Misc Order Settings (/cp/misc_order_settings/) for any cart display options before resorting to custom CSS.
H-08
No lifestyle photos on product pages -customers can't visualize the rental at their event
Product Pages Med Effort
The Problem
Product pages currently show catalog-style images of equipment on its own. Parents booking a bounce house for a kid's birthday party make emotionally-driven decisions — they want to picture it at their backyard party, see kids having fun, and feel confident it will look great. Stock-style product shots don't trigger that emotional response. Competitors who show real event photos create significantly stronger purchase intent.
The Fix
Add 2-3 real event photos to every product page — images of the item set up at an actual party, ideally with kids using it. These don't need to be professional; authentic iPhone shots from real deliveries perform well. Build a secondary image gallery below or alongside the primary product image. Prioritize high-traffic product pages first (water slides, bounce house combos, mechanical bull).
How to implement in ERS
  1. Upload event photos to Website > Gallery (/cp/gallery/). Name files descriptively before uploading (e.g., "water-slide-backyard-party-pearland.jpg") for SEO value.
  2. Go to Products > Items (/cp/items/), open each item, and update the item's image to your best real-event photo. ERS displays this as the primary product image on the listing and product page.
