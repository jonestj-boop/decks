The Core Problem
⚠️
The ecommerce store is invisible to Google. dogizone.etailpet.com is a subdomain of etailpet.com — not dogizone.com. Every product page, every category, every piece of content on that store builds authority for eTailPet's domain, not DogiZone's. DogiZone gets none of the SEO benefit from its own online store.

Why the subdomain problem matters beyond SEO

When a customer searches "dog food Rockville MD" or "bully sticks near me" and clicks a result that lands on dogizone.etailpet.com, they're on a URL that reads as a third-party platform — not DogiZone's own brand. For first-time visitors, it erodes trust. For repeat visitors, it creates a disjointed experience switching between dogizone.com for services and a different-looking subdomain for products. And from a paid media standpoint, running ads to a subdomain outside your main domain makes attribution, retargeting, and conversion tracking significantly more complex.

SEO Gaps — Current Store
Category and collection pages can't rank
Critical

Individual product URLs are clean and descriptive — /products/dogizone-slip-dog-lead-orange-2/ is fine. The problem is every category and brand collection page uses query parameter URLs: /products/list/?categories=0003000D for Dog Food, /products/list/?brand=1550 for brand pages.

Google treats query parameter URLs as low-priority, often skips them in crawls, and they carry no keyword signal. A "Dog Food Rockville MD" collection page can't exist in this URL structure. On Shopify: /shop/collections/dog-food-rockville/ — indexable, keyword-rich, rankable.

No product schema / rich snippets
Critical

The current store has no Product schema markup. Shopify generates this automatically and supports review stars, price, and availability in Google search results — significantly improving CTR from product searches.

Zero local product search visibility
Critical

Searches like "open farm dog food rockville md," "freeze dried dog food near me," and "bully sticks rockville" all have commercial intent and local competition DogiZone could win — but the current subdomain makes it impossible for dogizone.com to rank for any of them.

No "why we carry this" editorial layer
Medium

Product pages have solid manufacturer content — full ingredient lists, nutritional specs, and brand marketing copy. What's missing is DogiZone's own voice: why they chose to carry this brand, how staff recommends using it, which dogs it's best for. That editorial layer differentiates a specialty pet store from Chewy and builds SEO content that manufacturer descriptions don't cover.

No blog or buying guide content
High

A Shopify store on dogizone.com could host buying guides that rank organically and drive product discovery: "best freeze-dried dog food for senior dogs," "bully stick vs. rawhide — what's safer." These are high-intent, low-competition searches that feed directly into product purchases.

No Google Shopping integration
High

Shopify connects natively to Google Merchant Center for Shopping listings. DogiZone's products could appear in Google Shopping results — especially valuable for branded items like Open Farm and Steve's Real Food where local stock is a differentiator.

Local Delivery + Franchise Opportunity
Local delivery as a competitive differentiator
Opportunity

DogiZone already offers local delivery from the Rockville store. On Shopify, this becomes a full-featured, marketable offering with dedicated landing pages, local SEO content, and a checkout experience that clearly presents pickup vs. delivery options.

Content to build on dogizone.com/shop/: "same-day dog food delivery Rockville MD," "local pet supply delivery Bethesda" — these are low-competition searches with immediate commercial intent that Chewy and Amazon can't win locally.

Franchise scalability on Shopify
Opportunity

As DogiZone opens franchise locations, each location needs its own inventory, pickup option, and local delivery radius. Shopify's multi-location inventory management handles this natively — each franchise location can have its own stock levels, pickup availability, and local delivery zone within a single Shopify store.

On eTailPet, adding franchise locations to the existing subdomain creates brand fragmentation. On Shopify under dogizone.com, every location is part of the same brand, same domain, same authority.

Migration Roadmap
Phase 1 — Discovery & Setup
Export products, set up Shopify, configure domain
Export full product catalog from eTailPet. Set up Shopify store at dogizone.com/shop/ (subdirectory, not subdomain — critical for SEO). Select and configure a theme that matches the main dogizone.com brand. Migrate products with proper category structure, descriptions, and images. Set up GA4 integration under the existing DogiZone property so attribution is unified from day one.
Phase 2 — SEO Build
Keyword-optimize URLs, write product descriptions, add schema
Rename all product and collection URLs to keyword-rich slugs. Write or expand product descriptions — minimum 150 words per product, include brand name + use case + local angle where relevant. Enable Shopify's native Product and BreadcrumbList schema. Connect Google Merchant Center for Shopping listings. Submit sitemap to GSC. Build 5–10 buying guide blog posts targeting high-intent product searches.
Phase 3 — CRO Layer
Install email capture, reviews, abandoned cart, loyalty
Install Klaviyo for email + SMS. Set up welcome flow with first-order discount. Configure abandoned cart sequence (1hr email + 24hr SMS). Install Judge.me for product reviews — send post-purchase review requests to existing customer list. Set up Smile.io loyalty program with points for purchases and in-store visits. Add "frequently bought together" recommendations. Launch local pickup and local delivery prominently in checkout and on product pages.
Phase 4 — Franchise Prep
Multi-location inventory, location-specific content, subscription
Configure Shopify multi-location inventory for each franchise location as they come online. Build location-specific landing pages under dogizone.com/shop/[location]/ for local delivery SEO. Evaluate and launch a subscribe-and-save option for recurring purchases (food, treats) — highest LTV segment. Connect online and in-store loyalty so points earn across both channels.
💡
On migration: When the eTailPet store is retired, set up 301 redirects from all etailpet.com product URLs to the corresponding Shopify pages. This passes any existing link equity and prevents broken links from existing marketing materials. Notify all email lists and social channels of the new store URL at launch.
✓
What to tell the client: The current setup means your ecommerce store is working for eTailPet, not for DogiZone. Every sale you make online, every product page Google indexes, every customer who finds you through a product search — that's building equity on someone else's domain. Moving to Shopify under dogizone.com puts that equity where it belongs, ties your online store to your brand, and sets the foundation for selling products across every franchise location from a single platform.
