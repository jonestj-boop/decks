Executive Summary

What this audit covers

This report reviews all 104 blog articles across two OtterSpace content hubs: Essentials (23 articles) and Sleep Guides (81 articles). The analysis looks at content depth, publishing status, SEO signals, topic coverage, and structural opportunities that could improve organic visibility and on-site conversion.

The site has a strong content foundation with clear topical authority around sleep science, nursery setup, and blackout solutions. The primary opportunities are around content depth, unlocking draft inventory, and missing visual assets that are limiting indexability and engagement for a significant portion of the library.

Top Opportunities

55 articles are sitting as unpublished drafts
Critical

More than half the content library (55 of 104 articles) is not indexed by Google. These articles were written, uploaded to Shopify, but never published, which means they are generating zero organic traffic and zero revenue. Many of them are over 700 words and fully written.

Publishing even a portion of this backlog could significantly accelerate organic impressions without any new writing required.

Impact: Immediate indexation of up to 55 additional URLs. Fastest ROI on the table.
81 of 104 articles have no featured image
High Priority

Featured images are surfaced in Google Image Search, social previews (OG tags), and on-site blog listings. Articles without images appear as blank cards in blog grids, reducing click-through and time-on-site. This is especially impactful for a visually-driven product category like blackout window solutions.

Even a single branded lifestyle image per article would meaningfully improve how the blog presents across Google and social channels.

Impact: Improved CTR in Google Search image packs, social share previews, and on-site engagement.
35 articles are under 700 words, limiting search competitiveness
High Priority

Articles under 700 words often struggle to rank for informational queries because they cannot cover a topic with enough depth to satisfy search intent. Google's Helpful Content guidelines specifically reward articles that comprehensively answer the question a user typed in. Short articles risk being seen as "thin content" and may suppress rankings for the whole domain.

4 articles are under 400 words and are a particular concern. Expanding these to 700-900 words each with added FAQ sections, supporting tips, or product context would be a lightweight, high-value content update.

Impact: Better rankings for competitive informational queries. Stronger topical authority signals.
60 article titles contain internal notes or exceed 60 characters
Medium Priority

Titles are the single most important on-page SEO element. Several articles have internal production notes in their titles (e.g., "{pillar: baby sleeping on back}"), which appear in Google Search results and Shopify blog listings, making the site look unpolished to visitors and signals poor content governance to crawlers.

Additionally, many titles exceed 60 characters and will be truncated by Google in SERPs, reducing click-through rates. Tightening titles to 50-60 characters with a clear keyword and value proposition is a low-effort, high-signal improvement.

Impact: Cleaner SERP appearance, higher CTR, and removal of content quality flags.
Heavy topic concentration on nursery/baby with thin coverage elsewhere
Medium Priority

34 of 104 articles target nursery and baby sleep, creating deep coverage in one cluster. Meanwhile, high-intent categories like shift workers (6 articles), travel (5 articles), and gaming (8 articles) are underserved, despite these audiences being proven buyers of blackout solutions. Adding 4-6 well-targeted articles per underserved cluster could open new traffic channels with less keyword competition.

Impact: New organic traffic channels from high-converting but underserved audience segments.
Keyword overlap across nursery and sleep-science articles
Low Priority

Several high-frequency terms (baby, sleep, blackout, brain, curtain) appear across a large number of articles, with multiple posts targeting nearly identical informational queries. For example, there are several distinct articles on baby brain development, newborn sleep science, and REM sleep in infants that may compete with each other for the same search queries. A consolidation pass, or stronger internal linking between related pieces, would help Google understand which article is the canonical answer for each topic.

Impact: Stronger page authority on key topics, reduced keyword cannibalization risk.
