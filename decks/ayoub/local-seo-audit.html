Summary
After six months, the core metrics are moving in the right direction. GBP interactions have grown dramatically, local map rankings have improved on the most commercially valuable keywords, and the site is gaining organic search traction. However, several deliverable quality issues have emerged — particularly in the backlink program — and there are gaps in how the work is documented and verified. This audit covers what's working, what's slipping, and what needs attention going forward.

Strong AI Visibility Tracking

The reporting includes AI search presence tracking across ChatGPT, Google AI Overview, AI Mode, and Gemini — ahead of what most local SEO programs track. Ayoub N&H is appearing in AI-generated recommendations for Rockville carpet and rug cleaning queries.

Concern All Top GSC Queries Are Branded — Local Non-Branded Clicks Not Showing

API-verified GSC data for the campaign period shows that every high-click query is branded: "ayoub n&h" (218 clicks), "ayoub carpet" (51), "ayoub" (30), "ayoub carpet service" (21), "ayoub carpet cleaning" (17), "ayoub nh" (10). The first non-branded query by clicks is "oriental rug cleaning near me" at only 15 clicks (avg position 49.0 — barely visible). "Rug cleaning near me" gets 12 clicks at position 9.3. For a local SEO campaign running 6 months, non-branded local queries should be driving more meaningful click volume. The good news: the site does appear in these searches (672K impressions across the campaign period) — but CTR on non-branded commercial keywords is very low, suggesting positions are still too far down on the most competitive terms, or meta titles/descriptions need stronger optimization to pull clicks once in position.

Context Heavy paid advertising (83.5% of traffic) — organic growing but still 5% of sessions

API-verified GA4 data for the campaign period: Cross-network (Google/Meta ads) 81,974 sessions (83.5%), Direct 5,201 (5.3%), Organic Search 4,923 (5.0%), Display 2,307 (2.4%), Paid Search 1,977 (2.0%). Ayoub is running significant paid advertising — Cross-network alone accounts for 84% of all traffic. Organic search at 888 sessions/month in April 2026 is real and growing (up from 728 at campaign start, +22%), but it's a small fraction of a paid-heavy traffic mix. This context matters: the client isn't currently dependent on organic for lead volume, which reduces urgency but also means organic isn't yet the primary growth lever. As rankings continue to improve on non-branded local terms, organic share should grow.

On Track Content Pages Published

14 new pages were published across Months 2–6 (November 2025–April 2026), targeting relevant keywords with dedicated URLs. Pages are live and indexed. Highlights include the Rockville location landing page (now #1 and #3 in organic), plus dedicated service pages for drapery, Oriental rugs, upholstery, tile and grout, and hardwood. Localized subpages for Rockville (upholstery, area rug) were added in April.

What's Not Working / Concerns

Contract Shortfall 5 of 10 keywords not meeting the Top 3 scope guarantee

The scope guarantees Top 3 maps pack ranking across all 10 tracked keywords within a 5-mile radius. As of Month 6, 5 of 10 keywords are not meeting this guarantee:

  • Tile Cleaning — all N/A across the entire campaign; no maps visibility achieved
  • Hardwood Refinishing — flat at 1.23% Top 3 coverage (same as start)
  • Hardwood Floors — 0% → 1.23% (negligible; still 0% in maps for most of the grid)
  • Drapery Cleaning Services — 0% throughout; no Top 3 presence at any point
  • New Carpet Installation — actively declined from 60.49% to 45.68% (moving away from scope, not toward it)

The 5 keywords showing Top 3 presence (Carpet Cleaning, Rug Cleaning, Upholstery, Carpet Repair, Sofa Cleaning) are moving in the right direction but none has reached full grid coverage — the scope guarantee implies consistent Top 3 across the entire 5-mile radius, not partial coverage. This needs a specific remediation plan for each failing keyword.

Four domains have been reused across multiple months. Two of them (spaciodb.com and smallfeerealty.com) point to the exact same article URL in both December and February. A repeat link from the same page on the same domain provides negligible additional SEO value and should not count as a fresh deliverable.

Issue magnoliaspringshomes.com is a potentially toxic link — domain redirects to suspicious destination

This domain was used in both November (reported DR 30) and April (reported DR 19). The domain now redirects to a suspicious destination. This is not just a quality issue or a recycled-domain issue — a link from a domain that redirects to a spammy or unrelated site can pass harmful signals to the receiving domain, not just fail to provide value. Recommended actions: (1) determine what magnoliaspringshomes.com is currently redirecting to; (2) if the destination is spammy or unrelated, submit a Google disavow file for both placements; (3) neither the November nor April link should count as a valid deliverable — two replacement links from clean, compliant domains are owed for each month.

Concern Backlink Relevance Is Weak

Nearly all links are guest posts placed on topically unrelated sites — a government contracting firm, a real estate brokerage, a concrete company, a home builder, a risk management organization. While the articles themselves are carpet-cleaning-themed, the host sites have no topical connection to cleaning, home services, or the Rockville/DMV market. Google has become increasingly sophisticated at assessing topical relevance when evaluating link authority. These links count, but they're lower-value than genuine local or niche placements.

Concern January Content Appears to Be Duplicate

The January content deliverables list the same two page titles and identical URLs as the December deliverables:

  • "Carpet Cleaning Services in Rockville, MD" → ayoubnh.com/locations/rockville/
  • "Oriental & Area Rug Cleaning" → ayoubnh.com/cleaning/oriental-area-rug-cleaning/

If these are updates to existing pages rather than new pages, they should be labeled as such. Counting an update as one of the month's two deliverables is not consistent with the scope ("2 localized articles or pages per month").

What We Need to Be Checking

Verify Backlink DR Values

DR values in the deliverables sheet are self-reported by the contractor. DR fluctuates and should be independently verified via Ahrefs or SEMrush. More importantly, confirm whether the links are indexed and passing authority — not just whether they were placed.

Verify January Content Deliverables

Confirm whether the January pages were genuinely new pages or updates to existing December pages. If they're the same URLs, the month's content deliverable was not fulfilled per scope.

Verify Approval Process

The scope requires all work be submitted to the designated approval channel before implementation. There is no documentation in the reporting or deliverables sheet showing this process was followed. Approval records should exist for each content piece and backlink.

Flag Drapery Keyword Data — Wrong Market

The Month 6 report contains a ranking table labeled "Drapery cleaning ranking increases and new keywords" that lists Kensington, Docklands, Balmain, Belmont, Blacktown, Churchlands, and Yaroomba — all Australian suburbs, not Maryland locations. This data appears to have been pulled from the wrong account and needs to be explained and removed.

Verify GBP Post / FAQ / Image Activity

The scope calls for 4 GBP posts/month, 2 FAQs/month, and 2 images/month starting Month 2. None of this is tracked in the deliverables sheet or reporting PDF. Spot-checking the live GBP profile will confirm whether this cadence has been maintained.

Verify Technical SEO Changes

The scope calls for 5 technical SEO changes per month starting Month 2, drawn from the initial site audit. Neither the PDF nor the deliverables sheet documents what specific changes were made, on which pages, or when. A technical change log should exist.

Contract Shortfall Citations — no tracking documentation, 4 live directories still wrong after 6 months

No citation audit spreadsheet exists in Ayoub's deliverables — unlike PKA where the contractor at least produced an audit document (even if incomplete), Ayoub has no citation tracking at all. Overall Assessment
The GBP growth, organic position improvement (37.4 → 18.0), and AI visibility tracking are genuine wins. Five keywords are showing Top 3 presence and the content program has added meaningful pages. That said, this audit has identified multiple compliance gaps that need resolution: 5 of 10 keywords are not meeting the Top 3 guarantee, the backlink program has fallen below DR30 compliance since January, one domain (magnoliaspringshomes.com) appears to be redirecting to a suspicious destination and may need to be disavowed, and citation deliverables appear unexecuted — no citation tracking spreadsheet exists in the deliverables, and four actively indexed directories still show wrong phone numbers after 6 months of reported citation building. The underlying SEO trajectory is positive, but the gap between what was contracted and what was actually delivered is significant and needs to be addressed directly with the contractor.
