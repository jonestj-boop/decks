Content Audit

Writing Content ≠ Getting Cited by AI

LLMs retrieve and cite content differently than search engines. Most in-house teams are optimizing for the wrong signals.

What LLMs look for when citing a source

✓ Citation-Ready Format
Direct Q&A structure, clear definitions, FAQ schema markup, short declarative sentences, named entities (author, date, organization)
✗ Commonly Missing on In-House Content
No FAQ schema, no structured definitions block, headers written for humans not LLMs, no citation-anchor text, thin "about us" authority signals
⚠ The Gap
Great medical content gets written — then published without the structural layer that tells AI systems "this is the authoritative answer to X"

Typical page types and their AEO gaps

Page Type Common Gap Fix
Condition pages Missing FAQ schema + definition blocks
Blog posts Partial Q&A headers, TL;DR summaries
How-it-works Missing Step schema, numbered structure
About / Experts Missing Author schema, credentials markup
Comparison pages Missing Structured comparison tables + schema
The good news: These fixes don't require new content — they restructure and annotate what you've already written.
