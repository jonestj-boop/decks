## 2. Restricted Services — Require Leadership Involvement

The following services may NOT be pitched by the sales team without Josh and/or Deanna's direct involvement in scoping and pricing:

| Service | Why Restricted |
|---|---|
| **SEO traffic analysis / audit-driven pitches** | Requires custom data analysis, GSC/SEMrush deep dives, and strategic recommendations that are unique to each prospect. Cannot be templated. |
| **Website redesign (Webflow or other)** | Highly variable scope — page count, design complexity, CMS migration, and content requirements differ per client. Must be custom-scoped. |
| **GoHighLevel / CRM automation builds** | Pipeline design, automation sequences, integrations, and data migration vary dramatically. Requires discovery before quoting. |
| **Full omni-channel engagements (5+ services)** | When monthly agency fees exceed ~$8,000/mo or combine 5+ service lines, the engagement needs custom phasing, margin review, and operational planning. |

**Sales team CAN:**
- Identify prospects who need these services
- Present them as capabilities in a discovery call
- Collect requirements
- Prepare draft pricing, scope, deliverables, and timelines

**Before presenting to the client, sales team MUST:**
- Route the draft pitch to Josh and/or Deanna for review
- Get explicit approval on pricing, scope, and timelines
- Incorporate any revisions before sending

## 3. Pricing Guardrails

### 3A. Fixed Pricing Rules

- **AI Visibility packages (1A):** Prices are fixed at the three published tiers. No discounting.
- **Ad spend:** Always billed separately at cost. Never absorb ad spend into agency fees.
- **Management fees:** Never drop below $1,500/mo for paid media.

### 3B. Approved Discount Mechanisms

| Discount Type | Rules |
|---|---|
| **Quarterly prepay** | Maximum 12.5% discount on the quarterly total (e.g., $6,000 quarter → $5,250) |
| **Prepay bonus content** | Can offer bonus blog posts for prepaid retainers (e.g., 15 posts for 3-month prepay). Do NOT discount the monthly rate — add value instead. |
| **ClusterMagic volume pricing** | List price $1,900/mo for 40 articles. Can discount to $1,500/mo ONLY when bundled with 2+ other services. |
| **Bundle savings** | When combining 3+ services, total discount across all line items must not exceed 15% of the sum of individual prices. |

### 3C. What Requires Approval

Any of the following deviations from standard pricing require Josh and/or Deanna's sign-off before presenting to the client:

- Discounting any AI Visibility package tier below published price
- Dropping paid media management fee below $1,500/mo
- Offering a custom deliverable mix not defined in this document
- Extending payment terms beyond net-30
- Offering a "pilot" or "trial" period at reduced rates
- Modifying the scope of a standard package (e.g., "Can we do 7 AEO placements instead of 8?")

## 4. Engagement Structure Rules

### 4A. Approved Engagement Models

| Model | When to Use |
|---|---|
| **3-month commitment (default)** | The standard minimum commitment for all ongoing services. Lead with this. Longer commitments (6- or 12-month) are even better — offer additional value for longer terms. Frame as: "We earn the next 90 days by delivering results in the first 90." |
| **6- or 12-month commitment** | Preferred for larger engagements. Offer prepay discounts or bonus content to incentivize longer terms. |
| **Month-to-month retainer** | Negotiation tool only — use when a prospect is hesitant to commit. Not the default. |
| **Phased project (3–6 months)** | For engagements with a build phase (Month 1) + ongoing phase. Common for SEO + content combos. |
| **One-time sprint** | Landing page optimization, technical SEO cleanup, site migration. Must have defined deliverables and timeline. |

### 4B. Engagement Minimums

No minimum engagement size. If a client needs a smaller scope (e.g., $700/mo for 25 articles), that's fine — use the approved pricing tiers and sell at or above the published rates.

## 5. Pitch Content Guardrails

### 5A. Approved Claims and Language

**You MAY say:**
- "AI-powered content engine"
- "Strategy-led, data-driven"
- "One team managing [list of actual services being delivered]"
- "ClusterMagic content" (branded content engine)
- "Answer Engine Optimization" / "AEO"
- Reference trust logos: Biohm, Breyers, Char-Broil, Cloudflare, Dove Men+Care, Fruit of the Loom, Harvard, Jobcase, Staples, Techstars, Unilever, Y Combinator

**You MUST NOT say:**
- "Guaranteed results" or any language implying performance guarantees — **exception:** guaranteed maps pack ranking (top 3 within 90 days) is approved for Local SEO + GMR packages only (see Section 1B)
- Specific ROI numbers unless clearly labeled as "directional projections, not guarantees"
- "We'll get you to #1" or any organic ranking guarantees
- Claims about services not included in the engagement
- References to case studies or results that haven't been verified

### 5B. ROI Projections

- Projections are allowed but MUST include the disclaimer: "These are directional projections, not guarantees."
- Base projections on verifiable data (GSC impressions, SEMrush traffic values, current conversion rates)
- Never project revenue or leads without qualifying assumptions
- Use ranges, not point estimates (e.g., "$75K–$100K" not "$87K")

### 5C. Competitive Positioning

- Never name competitors in negative terms
- May reference competitive gaps found in research (e.g., "Low competition in paid social — only 2 active advertisers found")
- Position EmberTribe's differentiator as: AI-powered execution speed, ClusterMagic content volume, and AEO/Reddit-first strategy

## 6. Deliverable Definitions — What Each Item Actually Means

This section prevents scope ambiguity. When a deliverable appears in a pitch, it means exactly this and nothing more.

| Deliverable | Definition | Does NOT Include |
|---|---|---|
| **ClusterMagic article** | 1,200–2,000 word SEO blog post, published to client CMS, internally linked | Custom graphics, video, infographics, gated content |
| **AEO placement** | One Reddit thread interaction (comment on existing thread OR new seeded thread with coordinated responses) | Placements on other platforms (Quora, forums), paid Reddit ads |
| **Backlink (DA40+/DA50+)** | One contextual editorial link on a site meeting the DA threshold | Homepage links, footer links, PBN links, sponsored post labels |
| **Paid media campaign** | One audience/creative/channel combination, actively managed | Landing page builds, CRM integration, attribution modeling |
| **Local pillar page** | 1,500–2,500 word city+service page, SEO-optimized, published to CMS | Paid local listings, Google Business Profile management, citation building |
| **Hub page refresh** | Content update + SEO optimization of an existing metro/location page | Full page redesign, new URL structure, CMS migration |
| **Technical SEO implementation** | One discrete fix: schema markup, redirect, canonical tag, speed fix, etc. | Full site audit, CMS platform migration, server configuration |
| **On-page optimization** | Title tag, meta description, H-tag, and internal link updates for one existing page | Content rewrite, image optimization, page redesign |
| **GBP Management** | Ongoing Google Business Profile posts, photo/video uploads, monitoring, recommendations, rank tracking | Paid Google Ads, LSA management (separate add-on), review generation |
| **Guaranteed Maps Ranking (GMR)** | Top 3 ranking in Google Maps pack for 10–20 keywords within 90 days, within the specified mile radius (5mi or 10mi) | Organic search ranking guarantees, rankings outside the specified radius |
| **Localized content piece** | One local service page, service area page, or local blog post — SEO-optimized, published to CMS, 1 round of revision included | Custom graphics, video, gated content |
| **Local SEO backlink (DR 30+)** | One link from a DR 30+ site, localized and/or niche-relevant | DA40+/DA50+ editorial links (those are priced separately per Section 1A) |
| **LSA management** | Bid strategy optimization, lead monitoring via LSA dashboard, monthly performance reporting | Ad spend (billed separately at cost) |

## 8. Bespoke Engagement Process

When a prospect needs something outside the standard menu:

1. **Sales team identifies the need** during discovery
2. **Documents the requirements** (what the client wants, why, timeline, budget signals)
3. **Flags it as bespoke** and routes to Deanna with:
   - Prospect name and industry
   - Requested services (standard + non-standard)
   - Monthly budget range discussed
   - Timeline expectations
   - Any competitive pressure or urgency
4. **Josh/Deanna scopes and prices** the custom components
5. **Josh/Deanna confirms margins** are acceptable before the pitch is finalized
6. **Sales team delivers** the pitch with Josh/Deanna's approved pricing

**Never present a bespoke pitch to a client before Josh and/or Deanna has reviewed the final scope and pricing.**
